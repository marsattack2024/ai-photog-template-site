# Troubleshooting Google Ads for Photographers

Use this when the user says the campaign is not working, CPL is high, leads are bad, bookings are low, ads are disapproved, or traffic is not converting.

## Diagnose by Symptom

### High impressions, low CTR

Likely issues:
- Keywords are too broad.
- Headlines do not match search intent.
- Location/service is unclear.
- Competitors have stronger proof or offer.

Fixes:
- Tighten keywords.
- Add primary keyword to headlines.
- Add location and concrete service benefits.
- Improve extensions.
- Test stronger CTA and offer headlines.

### Good CTR, low conversion rate

Likely issues:
- Landing page mismatch.
- Weak mobile page.
- Form friction.
- Offer not clear enough.
- No proof or trust.

Fixes:
- Align landing page hero to ad promise.
- Shorten form.
- Add relevant testimonials.
- Improve CTA clarity.
- Test thank-you page and form submission.

### Low CPL, low bookings

Likely issues:
- Lead quality problem.
- Too broad keywords.
- Weak qualification.
- Slow follow-up.
- Offer attracts bargain shoppers.

Fixes:
- Add negatives.
- Use phrase/exact match.
- Improve form questions without making the form too long.
- Add consult scheduling.
- Strengthen follow-up sequence.

### High CPL, good bookings

Likely issues:
- Traffic may be expensive but profitable.
- Optimization should be based on ROI, not CPL alone.

Fixes:
- Calculate revenue, ad spend, booking rate, and average sale.
- Scale carefully if ROI is strong.
- Test more extensions and landing page variants.

### Ads disapproved

Likely issues:
- Sensitive boudoir imagery.
- Unsupported claims.
- Policy-triggering language.

Fixes:
- Use more modest image extensions.
- Remove exaggerated claims.
- Avoid overly sexual wording.
- Use private, empowering, guided, and tasteful language.

### Lead volume dropped suddenly

Check:
- Billing and account status.
- Ad approval status.
- Landing page live status.
- Form submission and thank-you page.
- Conversion tag firing.
- Search terms report.
- Competitor/seasonal changes.
- Budget limited status.

## Metrics That Matter

Track:
- Impressions
- CTR
- CPC
- Conversion rate
- CPL
- booked consult rate
- booking rate
- average sale
- revenue
- ROI

Do not optimize only for cheap leads. A $40 lead that books into a $2,500 sale beats a $7 lead that ghosts.

## Scaling Rule

Scale only when:

- Conversion tracking is working.
- Leads are entering the CRM.
- Follow-up is fast.
- The studio has capacity.
- Booking rate and average sale support the spend.

Increase budget gradually. Watch CPL, booking rate, and ROI together.
