# Stage 3 — Offer Stack

## Purpose

Build the three-tier offer for the close segment of this studio's specific event. The offer must be real, must create genuine scarcity, and must never cheapen the brand. No session discounts. No "50% off." Value-add offers only.

---

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — The Gift Voucher (Primary Offer — Run Every Time)

**What it is:** A complimentary session — the session fee is waived — for a hard-limited number of attendees who commit tonight. Products are at full investment. No discount on product pricing.

**Why it works:** The session fee is the perceived barrier, not the product investment. Waiving it feels like a significant gift (and it is — it's $150 to $500 in real value) without touching the revenue line that actually matters. The product sale is where the money lives.

**Quantity:** 3 maximum. Fewer if the studio's calendar is tight. The scarcity must be real — if everyone could get it, it isn't an offer, it's a policy.

**How to customize for this studio:**
- Use the studio's actual session fee from intake
- Name it something specific, not "free session" — examples: "The Complimentary Portrait Date," "The Studio Experience Pass," "Complimentary Session Night"
- Tie it to a specific booking window (e.g., "for any date in the next 60 days") to create urgency

**Script for the close:**
"Before tonight ends, I'm opening [3] session dates with no session fee — zero. You come in, you get the full experience, [include what's in the session fee, e.g., hair and makeup], and the session fee is completely on me. The only [3] women who get this are the [3] who book tonight. Once those dates are gone, they're gone."

---

### Tier 2 — The Artwork Credit (Floor Offer — Run Every Time)

**What it is:** A fixed dollar credit toward products — wall art, albums, prints, digital files — applied at the reveal/ordering appointment. Session fee still applies.

**Why it works:** Protects full pricing on the session. Rewards the room for being there. Creates urgency because the credit disappears when they walk out. It is the catch that converts everyone who didn't race for the Tier 1 dates.

**Amount:** $200 to $350 depending on the studio's average sale. Should feel meaningful — roughly 10-15% of average product investment.

**How to customize for this studio:**
- Set the credit at approximately 10% of the average sale from intake (e.g., $2,000 average sale → $200 credit)
- Name it: "Tonight-Only Artwork Credit" or "Preview Night Credit" — not "discount"
- Make clear it applies to wall art and albums, not the session fee

**Script for the close (immediately after Tier 1):**
"For everyone else who books tonight — not the [3] complimentary dates, those are taken — every woman who puts down a retainer before she leaves gets a $[X] credit toward her artwork order. That credit is gone when you walk out the door."

---

### Tier 3 — Participation Prize Upgrade (Engagement Tool — Optional)

**What it is:** Winners of the participation segment get a bonus add-on at their session. Small dollar value, high perceived personalization.

**Examples:** Extra outfit change, lash upgrade, extended HMU time (30 min), complimentary 5x7 print, digital download of one image.

**When to use:** Run this when the group seems reserved or hard to warm up. The competition element loosens the room and gets people invested before the close.

**When to skip:** If the room is already warm and engaged, you don't need it. The live shoot will do the work.

**Script during participation segment (not at the close):**
"If you win this round, that prize carries over into your session when you book tonight. So you'll want to pay attention."

**Key rule:** Announce prizes during the participation segment, not during the close. At the close, you reference only Tier 1 and Tier 2. Tier 3 is already in play.

---

## Offer Sequence at the Close

Present in this exact order. Never all at once.

1. Announce the Tier 1 complimentary dates. Pause. Watch who moves. Close those [3] first.
2. Immediately pivot: "For everyone else, anyone who books tonight locks in the $[X] artwork credit. That's the last offer on the table."
3. Open the floor: "I've got the booking form right here. Who's ready?"

**Rules:**
- Never discount further after announcing the offer
- Never extend the offer after the event ends ("I'll honor the credit through the weekend" kills the scarcity and trains the next room to wait)
- The credit must disappear at midnight or when they leave — the deadline is real or it means nothing

## Offer Stack Builder Output

For this studio, produce:

1. Tier 1 name and exact wording for the close script
2. Tier 1 quantity (default 3 — adjust if studio's calendar is constrained)
3. Tier 1 booking window (60 days recommended)
4. Tier 2 credit amount (calculated from average sale)
5. Tier 2 name
6. Tier 3 prize options (2-3 options for this genre)
7. The complete close script incorporating all three tiers

## Genre-Specific Offer Notes

**Boudoir:**
Tier 1 should include whatever is in the session fee — typically HMU. "Three dates with no session fee. Hair and makeup included. Zero out of pocket until your reveal."

**Newborn:**
Tier 1 should acknowledge the booking window constraint. "Three complimentary dates for sessions within 6 weeks of your due date. Session fee is on me."

**Family:**
Tie Tier 1 to a seasonal window. "Three complimentary fall portrait dates — September through November."

**Branding:**
Tier 1 framing is ROI-forward. "Three complimentary brand session dates. You invest in the products and walk away with a full content library."

**Senior:**
Tie Tier 1 to the senior's upcoming graduation year. "Three complimentary Class of [year] dates."
