# Stage 2 — Event Blueprint

## Purpose

Produce a custom event runsheet for this studio's specific genre, entry point, venue, and model situation. The blueprint is what the photographer hands to their team (or follows themselves) the night of the event.

---

## Event Parameters

Pull from intake context:
- genre
- entry_point
- event_date / venue
- has_past_client_model
- lead_source_size (cap RSVP at 15 seats regardless of list size)

---

## Standard Event Structure (2 to 2.5 hours)

Customize timing and content blocks to the studio's genre. The sequence never changes — only the content inside each block.

### [0:00 — 0:20] Arrival and Studio Tour

Guests arrive. Drinks and light refreshments are ready (wine, sparkling water, a charcuterie board if budget allows — the hospitality signals that this is not a sales pitch). The photographer or assistant walks small groups through the studio: client closet, lighting setups, product wall, HMU station.

**Goal:** Normalize the environment. Fear of boudoir (and portrait photography generally) is largely fear of the unknown. The studio tour dismantles it before the shoot begins.

**Host script during tour:** "This is where it all happens. Most women walk in nervous and walk out wondering why they waited this long. By the end of tonight you'll see exactly why."

**Genre note for newborn/maternity:** Show the safety equipment, posing props, temperature setup. Parents need to see that safety is built into the space, not explained over the phone.

---

### [0:20 — 0:30] Welcome and Frame-Setting

Short. The photographer introduces herself and sets the agenda. Two minutes maximum.

**Script model:**
"Thank you for being here. A lot of you have been curious about [boudoir/a session] for a while — maybe you filled out a form, maybe a friend brought you, maybe you just wanted to see what this is actually like. Whatever brought you, I'm glad you're here.

Tonight you're going to watch a real session happen in real time. Real posing. Real lighting. Real woman. Then we're going to look at the images together — you'll see what this actually produces. After that, I have something for anyone who decides tonight is her night.

Let's get started."

---

### [0:30 — 0:40] Participation Segment

Run a short Q&A or trivia round — 4 to 5 questions about the photography experience, common misconceptions, or the transformation process. Guests call out answers or hold up cards.

**Prize for correct answers:** Small and immediate. Print credits ($25-$50 toward their order), wardrobe vouchers, a free add-on (lash upgrade, extra outfit, extended HMU). Announce prizes carry over into their session when they book.

**Goal:** Get the room talking. People who participate become emotionally invested. The shoot that follows lands differently when they've already been active.

**Timing rule:** Cap at 10 minutes. This is warmup, not a quiz show.

**Genre-specific questions:**

*Boudoir:*
- "True or false: You need modeling experience to look good in boudoir photos."
- "What's the most common thing women say AFTER their session that they never expected to feel?"
- "How many outfit changes does a typical session include?"

*Newborn:*
- "What's the ideal age for a newborn session — and why does it matter?"
- "True or false: Newborn posing requires the baby to be fully asleep."
- "Name one thing parents are always surprised they don't need to bring."

*Family:*
- "What's the one thing that ruins more family portrait sessions than anything else?"
- "How far in advance should you book a fall family session?"
- "True or false: Kids need to be perfectly behaved for a great family portrait."

*Branding:*
- "What's the single biggest mistake business owners make with their headshots?"
- "How many pieces of content can one brand shoot realistically produce?"
- "True or false: You need to have a polished personal brand before booking a shoot."

---

### [0:40 — 1:10] Live Shoot (30 minutes)

The photographer shoots the model in front of the room. This is the core of the event. Nothing replaces it.

**What to cover:**
- One before/after posing sequence — show the difference between unposed and directed
- Two to three lighting setups or transitions
- Two to three outfit changes (boudoir/branding/family) or setup changes (newborn)
- Narrate throughout: "Notice what I'm doing with her chin here." "Watch how the light changes when I turn her toward the window." "This is the exact pose that makes every woman feel like she belongs in front of a camera."

**Model brief (prepare this before the event):**
Tell the model ahead of time: be herself. Nervous at the start is fine — even better. The transformation from nervous to confident, visible in real time, is the sales argument. A past client who can briefly say "I was terrified when I walked in for mine, and then..." before the shoot is worth more than any testimonial on a website.

**If no past client model is available:** A friend, neighbor, or referral works. Brief her the same way. The shoot still demonstrates the posing, the lighting, and the transformation.

---

### [1:10 — 1:35] Live Image Reveal

Pull the images on the large screen while the model and guests are still in the room. Unedited or lightly edited — the goal is immediacy, not perfection.

Walk through 12-15 selects. Narrate briefly for each one.

**Then: product moment.** Put an album in someone's hands. Point to the canvas on the wall. Walk them to the product display and let them touch things. Do a room mockup if capable — "If you put this image here in a 24x36, this is what it looks like on a standard bedroom wall."

**Goal:** This is your IPS setup happening before the booking, not after. By the time the offer comes, they've already held the product, pictured it on their wall, and watched themselves imagining it. The product reveal is not a sales presentation. It is a demonstration.

**Model reveal note:** If the model is a past client, let her react to her own images in front of the room. Authentic reaction — surprise, emotion, confidence — closes faster than anything you can script.

---

### [1:35 — 1:50] The Close

Present the offer stack in sequence. Load `stages/03-offer-stack.md` for the customized offer built in Stage 3.

**Delivery rule:** Warm, confident, no apology. You are offering these women something valuable because they showed up. This is not a sales pitch — it is a reward for being in the room.

---

### [1:50 — 2:10] One-on-One Time

After the formal close, stay available. Have booking forms and a card reader or payment link ready. Some women will not raise their hand in a group but will approach privately. Give them that space. Do not rush the room out.

Assistant or second team member circulates, answers questions, helps with forms.
