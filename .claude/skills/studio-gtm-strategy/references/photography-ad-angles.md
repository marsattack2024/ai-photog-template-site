# Photography Ad Angles — Hook Banks & Templates

## The Core Rule

Don't sell the product (a photography session). Sell the benefit (how she'll feel, what she'll have, what fear disappears). "Newborn photographer near me" is a commodity. "You bring the baby — we handle everything else" is a benefit.

## Hook Formula Library

### 1. The Objection-Flipper
Takes the #1 fear and makes it the reason to book.
- "Every woman who's booked with us had something she wanted to change first."
- "She said 'my kids never cooperate.' Then she saw the photos."
- "You don't need to lose the weight. You need to see yourself differently."
- "He said he hates photos. Then he saw the portraits and teared up."

### 2. The Urgency Hook
Creates time-based motivation without fake scarcity.
- "Your kids are 3 and 5 right now. Not 3 and 5 forever."
- "The newborn window is 5-14 days. This version of tiny doesn't wait."
- "She takes 50 photos a day of her kids. She's in none of them."
- "One day you'll wish you had these. Today you still can."

### 3. The Social Proof Hook
Leads with a real result or number.
- "500+ women. Every size. Every age. Zero regrets."
- "[X] five-star reviews. Here's why [city] trusts [Studio]."
- "She walked in nervous. She walked out crying happy tears."
- "[Client name] said 'I wish I'd done this years ago.' Here's her story."

### 4. The Curiosity Hook
Creates an information gap she needs to close.
- "The #1 thing [women/moms/brides] get wrong about [genre] photography."
- "What nobody tells you about your [boudoir/family/newborn] session."
- "3 things your photographer should include for free (most don't)."
- "The question every [genre] client asks — and the answer that changes everything."

### 5. The Contrarian Hook
Challenges an assumption.
- "You don't need to be photogenic. You need the right photographer."
- "Stop waiting until you 'feel ready.' Nobody ever does."
- "The best family photos aren't the ones where everyone's smiling."
- "Your phone photos aren't enough. Here's why."

### 6. The Story Hook
Opens a narrative arc.
- "6 months ago, [Name] almost didn't book because [objection]. Today..."
- "When she saw her images for the first time, she couldn't speak."
- "I photographed a woman who hadn't felt beautiful in 15 years. Then this happened."
- "A dad who 'hates photos' walked into our studio. What happened next..."

## Ad Body Templates

### The Objection-Flipper Ad
```
[HOOK — objection in her words, under 12 words]

[2-3 sentences flipping it with this studio's specific answer]

[1 sentence of proof: client quote, number, or result]

Take our 60-second quiz and claim your $[X] session credit →

---
Headline (40 chars): [Benefit-driven]
Description (30 chars): [Take the Quiz — $X Credit]
CTA: Learn More
```

### The Quiz/Door-Opener Ad
```
[HOOK — curiosity or benefit]

[What she'll learn or earn by taking the quiz]

[Brief credibility: reviews, clients served, years]

4 questions. 60 seconds. A $[X] credit waiting at the end.

---
Headline: Claim Your $[X] Session Credit
Description: 4 Questions. 60 Seconds.
CTA: Take the Quiz
```

### The Video Testimonial Ad
```
Caption: "[One-sentence result] — hear [Name]'s story."

[Video is the creative — phone-shot, vertical, authentic]

Ready for your own experience? Take the quiz → [LINK]
```

### The Retargeting Ad (3 audiences)

**Visited site/quiz, didn't complete:**
"Still thinking about it? Most of our favorite clients took a few days. Your $[X] credit is waiting: [LINK]"

**Started quiz, didn't finish:**
"You were one question away from your $[X] credit. Pick up where you left off: [LINK]"

**Booked consult, no-showed:**
"Life happens — we saved your spot. Reschedule in 30 seconds: [LINK]"

## Retargeting Carousel (10 Slides)

Adapt topics to the genre. One benefit or objection-flip per slide:

1. Posing guidance included
2. Hair & makeup / wardrobe / props included
3. Privacy commitment (boudoir) or kid-friendly process (family)
4. Payment plans available
5. Client testimonial screenshot
6. Before/after or reveal reaction
7. Products: wall art, albums, prints
8. Google rating + review count
9. Studio interior or behind-the-scenes
10. CTA: "Claim your $[X] credit → Take the Quiz"

## The Hook Bank Testing Method

For top 2 angles:
1. Write 5 different hooks
2. Keep the exact same body copy, offer, and CTA
3. Run all 5 simultaneously
4. Kill losers after 3-5 days (look at CTR and CPL)
5. Scale the winner
6. Repeat with new hooks monthly

The hook is 80% of performance. Don't test body copy. Test hooks.

## Platform Rules

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- First 125 chars = standalone pitch (before "See More" cutoff)
- Mobile-first. Most views are on phones.
- Benefits not features. "Follow-up that runs while you sleep" not "automated sequences."
- Single CTA per ad.
- Conversational tone. Sixth-grade reading level.
- Specificity beats cleverness. "$30K month" beats "amazing growth."
- Native-feeling creative outperforms polished stock.
- Your own portfolio photos ARE the creative. Use them.

### Google (Search — Timeline Genres)
- Match search intent precisely
- RSA: 8-10 headlines (max 30 chars each), 4 descriptions (max 90 chars each)
- Each headline independently coherent
- Mix: problem, solution, benefit, brand, CTA, proof, urgency, location
- Landing page must match the ad's promise
- Pin sparingly. Let Google optimize.

## Creative Direction Notes

- Use YOUR portfolio images. Not stock. Not other photographers' work.
- Vertical video outperforms horizontal on Meta.
- Authentic > polished. Phone-shot behind-the-scenes often beats studio portfolio shots.
- Carousel of client work with caption story = high engagement.
- Seasonal creative refreshes every 4-6 weeks prevent fatigue.
- Best-performing organic posts = free ad tests. Boost what already works.
