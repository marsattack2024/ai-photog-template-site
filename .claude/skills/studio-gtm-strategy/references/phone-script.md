# The 5-Stage Phone Script — Quick Reference Card

Print this. Keep it next to your phone. Follow the ORDER, not the exact words.

## Pre-Call Ritual (5 min before every call)

1. Quiet room, no interruptions
2. Headphones or speaker — hands free
3. Record the call (ask permission)
4. Pen + physical notepad
5. Close laptop — you're listening
6. Release attachment to the "yes"
7. Dial the SECOND the clock hits

## Stage 1: Rapport (30 seconds)

Three questions. That's it.

- "Hey [Name], how's your week going?"
- "Where are you calling from today?"
- "By the way — where did you find me?"

Don't skip it. Don't spend ten minutes on it.

## Stage 2: Agenda (30 seconds)

Take control. Don't let them drive.

"Before we get started — I'd like to ask you a few questions about you and your vision for the session. If I think we're the right fit, I'll tell you exactly how to reserve your spot. Sound good?"

"If I THINK we're the right fit" = prestige. You're the expert deciding.

## Stage 3: Discovery (10-15 minutes)

This is the close. Diagnose perfectly and the prescription is obvious.

### Universal Questions (every genre)
- What made you start thinking about this?
- Have you done anything like this before?
- What would it mean to you to have these images?
- Is there anything about the experience that makes you nervous?
- Where in your home would you imagine hanging these?
- What would it mean to walk past that image every morning?

### Boudoir
- Is this for you, or a gift for someone?
- What would seeing yourself in those images mean to you?
- Have you thought about display vs. private keepsake?

### Newborn / Maternity
- When are you due? Congratulations!
- Is this your first? Tell me about your family.
- Other little ones? What are their personalities like?
- Lifestyle or more posed — what are you drawn to?
- Family heirloom or meaningful item to include?

### Family
- Tell me about your family — ages?
- Who's the ham, who's the shy one?
- Last time you had professional photos?
- Main reason you're thinking about it now?
- Anyone else in the session — grandparents, pets?

### Wedding / Engagement
- Tell me about the day — what's the vibe?
- What's most important to you about the photography?
- Non-negotiable moments to capture?
- Date and venue?

### Headshot / Branding
- What do you need the images for?
- Brand vibe — corporate, creative, or in between?
- Individual, team, or both?
- When do you need them by?

**THE KEY:** Ask. Then LISTEN. Write down their exact words. Mirror them back in the prescription.

## Stage 4: Prescription (3 minutes)

Keep it short. Discovery did the work.

"Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd recommend..."

- Use THEIR words, not your marketing language
- Reference the offer by name
- Walk through what's included
- State the session fee clearly
- Introduce IPS: "Most clients invest between $[range] in their final collection — wall art, albums, prints. Payment plans available."

DO NOT hide the IPS. Set expectations.

## Stage 5: Close (2 minutes)

**Standard:** "I'd love to get you on the calendar. I have [date] and [date] — which works?"

Two specific dates. Not "when are you free?"

**Prestige:** "I only take [X] sessions per month. I have [Y] spots left in [month]. Want one?"

### Objection Handlers

**"I need to think about it."**
"Totally understand. What specifically are you weighing? I'd rather help you work through it now."

**"Too expensive."**
"I hear you. We have payment plans — [X] payments of $[Y]. And the collection at the reveal is completely up to you. No pressure."

**"Need to ask my partner."**
"Of course! What will they want to know? I can send a summary or do a quick 5-min call with both of you."

**"Not ready yet."**
"Totally fine. When's better? I'll set a reminder. Your credit doesn't expire."

**If yes:**
"Amazing! I'm sending a confirmation with everything — what to expect, how to prepare, your date. You'll also get a pre-session questionnaire so I can start planning."

Always end warm. Non-bookers become referrals and future clients.

## The Recording Rule

Record every call. Listen back weekly. This alone can double bookings. You can't fix what you can't hear.

## The Energy Rule

Words don't book sessions. Tonality does. If you sound unsure, they feel unsure. If you sound like you've done this a thousand times and you're genuinely thrilled to help — they trust you before you've asked a question.
