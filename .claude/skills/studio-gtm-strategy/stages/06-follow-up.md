# Stage 6: Pre-Call System & Follow-Up Sequences

## The Speed-to-Lead Rule

First contact within 5 minutes. Studios responding in under 5 minutes convert at dramatically higher rates than those waiting 30+ minutes. Message 1 is always automated and instant.

The photographer who responds first usually wins — not the best photographer. We've seen this repeatedly: the studio that shows up first gets the booking.

## The Pre-Call System (Before the Phone Rings)

This is the invisible machine that warms leads between inquiry and consultation. By the time the phone rings, the prospect should already know the photographer's face, have seen testimonials, understand the process, and have context on investment.

### Touch Point 1: Instant (T+0) — Automated

**SMS (immediate):**
"Hey [Name], it's [Photographer] from [Studio]! I just saw your inquiry come through — I'm calling you in a few minutes. Talk soon!"

**Email (immediate):**
Subject: "You're in, [Name]!"
Body: Confirm what they signed up for. Restate the offer. Under 100 words. Include: "I'm going to call you shortly — pick up!"

**Important:** Be careful with links in first text messages — carriers can flag them as spam. Lead with a question or a personal note. Get a reply first. Your scheduler link goes in the email and thank-you page.

### Touch Point 2: The Call (T+2-5 minutes)

Call them. If no answer:
- Leave a voicemail (15 seconds, warm, mention you saw their inquiry)
- Send follow-up text: "Hey [Name], just tried calling! When's a good time to chat about your [session type]?"

### Touch Point 3: Thank-You Page (seen immediately after quiz/form completion)

The thank-you page is one of the highest-leverage assets in the system:
- **Video of the photographer** (60-90 seconds): face to camera, introduce yourself, explain what happens next, build trust, show personality
- **Scheduling widget** (Calendly, Cal.com, GHL calendar) for booking the consultation
- **Investment context:** "Most clients invest between $[X] and $[Y] in their portraits. Payment plans available."
- **Social proof:** 1-2 testimonial quotes + photos
- **What to expect:** Brief walkthrough of the process

### Touch Point 4: The Call-Text-Call Cadence

If they don't answer the first call, follow this cadence:
- Day 1: Call, text, call (the 5x7 pattern — 5 attempts over 7 days)
- Day 2: Text with a question ("Still interested? Would love to chat!")
- Day 3: Call again
- Day 5: Email with value (testimonial or behind-the-scenes)
- Day 7: Final attempt text ("Hey — I don't want to bug you. If you're still thinking about it, I'm here. If not, no worries at all!")

## Track 1: The Conversion Sequence (Hot/Warm Leads — 7 Messages, 10 Days)

For leads who completed the quiz and/or have a consultation scheduled. Mix of email and SMS.

### Message 1 — Instant Confirmation (Email + SMS, T+0)
**Email subject:** "You're in, [Name] — here's what happens next"
**Email:** Confirm the quiz completion and offer. Consultation booking link. Under 100 words. "Tomorrow I'm sending you something special — a look at what a [genre] session actually looks like. Keep an eye out."
**SMS:** "Hey [Name], it's [Photographer] from [Studio]! Thanks for taking the quiz — I'm calling you shortly!"

### Message 2 — The Behind-the-Scenes Email (T+24h)
**Subject:** "Here's what a [genre] session actually looks like"
**Body:** Walk them through the experience. What happens before, during, and after. Show the USP elements (wardrobe, styling, guided posing, reveal). Include 2-3 images of past sessions (or image placeholders). This is NOT a sales email — it's trust-building through transparency. End with: "P.S. — I saved a spot for you this [week/month]: [LINK]"

### Message 3 — Proof Story (SMS, T+48h)
"[Name], quick story — [client description] came in nervous about [objection]. She left saying [specific quote or result]. Want to see if we can create that for you? [LINK]"

### Message 4 — Objection Killer (Email, T+72h)
**Subject:** "Wondering if this is really for someone like you?"
**Body:** Address the #1 genre objection using Story Proof format. "[Client name/description] felt the same way. Here's what happened..." End with consultation link.

### Message 5 — The Investment Email (T+5 days)
**Subject:** "What to expect when you invest in [genre] portraits"
**Body:** Frame the investment. "Most clients invest between $[X] and $[Y] and choose a combination of wall art, albums, and digital images. Payment plans available through [PayPal Credit/Affirm]." This email prevents sticker shock on the phone. Include a product showcase or gallery link.

### Message 6 — Transformation Story (Email, T+7 days)
**Subject:** "From 'I'm so nervous' to 'this is the best thing I've ever done'"
**Body:** Mini case study. 150-200 words. Specific feelings and outcomes. The arc: how she felt before -> what the experience was like -> how she felt after -> what she chose for her home.

### Message 7 — The Breakup (Email, T+10 days)
**Subject:** "Should I close your file?"
**Body:** "I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back — no hard feelings. If now isn't the right time, I'll step back. But if you're still thinking about [genre] photos, here's one last spot: [LINK]. Either way, wishing you the best. — [Photographer Name]"

The breakup email consistently has the highest reply rate because it triggers loss aversion.

## Track 2: The Nurture Sequence (Cold/Long-Term Leads — 8 Messages, 4 Weeks)

For leads that completed the Conversion Track without booking, OR leads who indicated "just exploring" on the quiz. **80% value, 20% ask.** Email only — no SMS.

### Nurture 1 (Week 1): "The biggest myth about [genre] photography"
Bust a common misconception. Provide the correct framework. Soft CTA.

### Nurture 2 (Week 1): "3 questions to ask any [genre] photographer before you book"
Teach them how to evaluate studios — positioning this studio favorably without being salesy.

### Nurture 3 (Week 2): "What [happy client] wishes she'd known before her session"
Client perspective story. Not "hire us" — "here's what the experience is actually like."

### Nurture 4 (Week 2): "[Genre] session prep: what to do (and what NOT to do)"
Practical value. Outfit tips, timing advice, prep checklist. Demonstrates expertise.

### Nurture 5 (Week 3): "The one thing I tell every [client type] before her session"
Personal insight from the photographer. Builds connection and trust.

### Nurture 6 (Week 3): "I made this for you: [free resource]"
A style guide, a "what to expect" PDF, a prep checklist, or a gallery of inspiration images.

### Nurture 7 (Week 4): "Quick question"
Short email. "I'm curious — what's the one thing holding you back from booking your [genre] session?" Re-engages and provides intel.

### Nurture 8 (Week 4): "Last one from me (unless you want more)"
"I've been sending you [genre] tips for a month. If any of this was helpful, I'd love to chat about your session: [LINK]. If not, no hard feelings. Reply 'keep going' if you want to stay on my list."

## The Reactivation Play ($20K Friday)

For photographers with existing dead leads in their CRM, phone, or email list:

"Send this text to every lead from the last 6 months who didn't book: 'Hey [Name], it's [Photographer]. Are you still interested in [genre] photos? I have a few openings this month.' That's it. You'll be surprised how many respond."

This exercise alone has produced $20K+ weekends for studios with large lead lists.

## Channel Rules

**SMS:** Under 160 chars, first name, conversational, business hours only (9 AM-7 PM), max 1 per 48h, questions over links.
**Email:** Subject under 50 chars, plain text outperforms HTML for sequences, one CTA, under 200 words, from photographer's name not studio brand, 6th-8th grade reading level.
