# Stage 3: Messaging & Brand Voice Framework

## Positioning Statement

Formula: "We help [specific woman/person] experience [specific transformation] through [unique mechanism], even if [biggest objection]."

Generate 2-3 variations. Let the photographer pick or combine.

**The Scroll Test:** Read the positioning statement as if it appeared as the first line of a Facebook ad in your feed. Does it stop the scroll? If not, it's too generic. A positioning statement that doesn't work as an ad hook needs to be sharper. Rewrite until it passes.

**Photography-specific test:** Could this statement apply to any other studio in their market? If yes, it's not specific enough. The USP differentiators from Stage 2 must be visible in the positioning.

## The 3 AM Thoughts Exercise

This is one of the most powerful messaging tools in the entire system. Ask the photographer:

"What is your ideal client thinking about at 3 AM that relates to what you do?"

Genre examples:
- **Boudoir:** "I haven't felt attractive since before the kids." "I keep saying I'll do it when I lose weight, but I never lose the weight." "I want to feel like ME again, not just 'mom.'"
- **Newborn:** "She's already two weeks old and I haven't scheduled photos." "What if the photographer isn't careful with my baby?" "I look terrible — I just had a baby."
- **Family:** "My kids are growing so fast and I have zero professional photos." "Every photographer we've tried couldn't handle my kids." "I'm never IN the photos."
- **Wedding:** "What if they miss the important moments?" "I don't want to look back and regret cheap photos." "Everyone says book early but how early?"
- **Headshot/Branding:** "My headshot is from 2019 and I look completely different." "I don't have a 'brand' yet." "I hate how I look in photos."

These thoughts become ad hooks, email subject lines, and quiz question angles. They're more powerful than any feature list because they mirror the exact internal conversation the buyer is having.

## Messaging Hierarchy

**Level 1 — Brand Promise (1 sentence):** The core transformation. Used in headlines, taglines, elevator pitches. Built from the USP statement in Stage 2.

**Level 2 — Supporting Claims (3-5 sentences):** Each addresses a key benefit or overcomes an objection. Used in subheadlines, bullets, ad copy. Pull from the "So What?" transformations in Stage 2.

**Level 3 — Proof Points:** Specific client results, testimonial quotes, credentials, before/after metrics. Use the social proof inventory from Stage 1. If specific proof isn't available, mark as [TODO: collect — e.g., "X clients photographed" or "Y-star Google rating"] only if photographer says to skip.

**Level 4 — Voice Personality:** 3 adjectives. Then provide 2 "say this / don't say this" examples for EACH adjective so the voice is actionable, not abstract.

**Default voice for photography studios:** Warm, confident, encouraging. The audience is predominantly women. Direct but never aggressive. Coach energy, not salesperson energy. Think: your best friend who happens to be really good at this.

Example:
- Adjective: "Warm"
- Say: "I can't wait to show you what you look like through my lens."
- Don't say: "Book your professional portrait session today."

- Adjective: "Confident"
- Say: "I've photographed 500+ women and every single one said the same thing: 'I was so nervous, and it was the best experience of my life.'"
- Don't say: "We think we might be able to help you feel better about yourself."

- Adjective: "Real"
- Say: "You don't need to lose weight, buy lingerie, or figure out how to pose. That's my job."
- Don't say: "Our team of experienced professionals will ensure a seamless photography experience."

## Objection-Handling Map

For each top 5 objections from the Studio Intelligence Brief:

| Objection | Reframe | Studio-Specific Proof | Ad Hook | Quiz Statement Angle | Story Proof |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| [objection] | [reposition as reason to book] | [this studio's answer] | [1-sentence ad hook] | [how this becomes a quiz statement slide] | ["I almost didn't because [objection]. But then [result]."] |

The **Story Proof** column formats the objection as a testimonial-style narrative for Proof Ads.
The **Quiz Statement Angle** column maps directly to the Quiz Inquiry Machine questions in Stage 5.

This table is the single most reusable asset in the system — it feeds ads, quiz, landing page, follow-up emails, and phone script.
