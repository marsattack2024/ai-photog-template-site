# Stage 1: Studio Discovery & Market Intelligence

## Step 1: Website Audit (DO THIS FIRST)

When the photographer provides their URL, immediately fetch it with web_fetch. Do not ask all intake questions first — audit the site, then ask targeted questions based on what you find.

**Audit checklist:**
- [ ] Genre clarity: Is it obvious what they shoot within 3 seconds?
- [ ] CTA presence: Is there a clear "book now" / "inquire" / "take the quiz" button above the fold?
- [ ] Pricing visibility: Is pricing listed? (If yes and they're doing IPS, flag this — pricing on site kills IPS studios)
- [ ] Quiz/popup: Any Typeform embed, exit popup, or interactive lead capture?
- [ ] Testimonials: Real client quotes with names/photos? Or nothing?
- [ ] About page: Generic bio or compelling story that builds trust?
- [ ] Social proof: Reviews, press mentions, credentials, client count?
- [ ] Portfolio: Quality of work displayed? Consistent genre or scattered?
- [ ] Mobile experience: Does it work on mobile? (Most traffic is mobile)
- [ ] Copy quality: Generic ("professional photographer") or specific ("luxury boudoir experiences for women who've never felt beautiful in photos")?

**Extract and save:**
- Any positioning statements, taglines, or headlines
- Testimonial quotes (exact words — these are gold for messaging)
- About page story elements (origin, why they do this, personal details)
- Product/service mentions (wall art, albums, digitals, collections)
- Any offers currently running

Present findings: "Here's what I found on your site — [strengths], [gaps]. A few questions to fill in what I couldn't see..."

## Step 2: Competitor Scan

If web search is available:

1. Search "[genre] photographer [city]" — note top 3-5 results
2. Quick-scan competitor sites for: positioning, pricing visibility, offers, lead capture, testimonial quality
3. Search "[genre] photographer [city] facebook ads" for Meta Ad Library presence
4. Note: what angles are overplayed in this market, and where the gap is

**Do not spend more than 3-5 minutes on this.** The goal is to identify the competitive landscape, not do exhaustive research. Summarize in 3-4 sentences.

## Step 3: Intake Questions

Present in 2 batches. Skip questions already answered by the website audit.

**Batch 1 — Your Studio:**
1. What genre do you shoot primarily? (If multiple: "Which ONE would you focus all your marketing on for the next 90 days?")
2. How long have you been shooting professionally?
3. Where are you located? Do clients come to a studio, or do you shoot on location?
4. What's your session fee? What does a typical client spend total (including products)?
5. Do you sell products in person (IPS/reveal session), or do you deliver digitals?
6. How are you currently getting clients? (referrals, ads, organic social, word of mouth, nothing)
7. Roughly how many inquiries do you get per month?
8. Of those, what percentage book? (If unknown: "How many sessions do you shoot per month?")

**Batch 2 — Your Market & Goals:**
9. What do your happiest clients say about working with you? (exact words if they remember)
10. What's different about your studio vs. the other [genre] photographers in your area? (Probe deep — don't accept "my photography" as an answer. Ask: "What do clients experience with you that they can't get anywhere else?")
11. What's one thing about your studio, your process, or your space that clients always comment on?
12. Monthly ad budget: $0 / Under $500 / $500-$1,500 / $1,500-$3,000 / $3,000+ / Not sure
13. Do you have testimonials, Google reviews, before/after photos, or video testimonials?
14. What does success look like in 90 days? (number of sessions, revenue target, specific goal)

**Freedom level: HIGH.** Probe deeper on questions 9, 10, 11, and 13 — these are the engine of the USP. If they give vague answers, push: "Give me a specific moment. A specific client. What happened?"

## Step 4: Find Your Stage

Based on their answers, classify them into one of five stages. This determines which parts of the system to prioritize.

**Stage 1: "Nobody knows I exist."**
Skills but no pipeline. No one is finding them. Need visibility and prospecting first.
-> Priority: Prospecting strategy, basic offer, quiz funnel, organic social plan

**Stage 2: "People visit but don't inquire."**
Getting some attention — website traffic, social followers — but not converting to conversations. Website, offer, or trust signals have a leak.
-> Priority: Website fixes, Quiz Inquiry Machine, offer development, USP clarity

**Stage 3: "I get leads but can't book them."**
Leads come in but ghost, stall, or say "too expensive." Follow-up, phone process, or consultation has a hole.
-> Priority: Phone script, pre-call system, follow-up sequences, speed-to-lead

**Stage 4: "I book clients but don't make enough per session."**
Calendar has people but revenue doesn't match the work. Session fee, reveal process, or product menu needs attention.
-> Priority: IPS/reveal system, offer restructuring, pricing strategy, product menu

**Stage 5: "I'm maxed out and exhausted."**
Full calendar, decent revenue, but they're the bottleneck. Need systems, delegation, maybe first hire.
-> Priority: Automation, hiring, efficiency — the GTM system still helps but capacity is the constraint

Tell the photographer: "Based on what you've told me, you're at Stage [X]. That means we're going to focus the system on [priority]. Everything else still gets built, but [priority] is where we'll see the biggest impact first."

## Step 7: Social Proof Inventory

Document what's available:
- Google reviews (number and average rating)
- Testimonial quotes (from website + what they shared)
- Before/after photos or galleries
- Video testimonials
- Press mentions or features
- Client count or years of experience
- Revenue or booking metrics they're willing to share

Note what's missing. Social proof is the highest-leverage asset — a specific client result outperforms any amount of clever copywriting.
