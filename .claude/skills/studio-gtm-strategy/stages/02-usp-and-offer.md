# Stage 2: USP Development & Offer Architecture

This is the most important stage in the entire build. Everything downstream — ads, quiz, landing page, phone script — runs on the USP. If this is generic, everything is generic.

## The Core Problem

Every portrait photographer in every market says the same things: "professional photographer," "capturing your special moments," "now booking sessions." When a prospect searches Google or scrolls Facebook, they see twenty versions of the same studio. Same language. Same offers. Same look.

The USP is what breaks them out of the lineup. It's the answer to: "Why should I book with YOU and not anyone else?"

## Step 1: USP Excavation

Load `references/usp-excavation.md` for the full guided process.

**The Three Layers:**

### Layer 1: The Studio Experience (What They Get That's Different)
Probe with specific questions — do not accept "my photography" as an answer:

- "Walk me through what happens from the moment a client walks in your door to the moment they leave."
- "What do you provide that most studios charge extra for — or don't offer at all?"
- "What's physically in your studio that clients comment on?" (wardrobe closet, hair/makeup station, product samples, specific backdrops, props)
- "How long is your session vs. typical in your market?"
- "Do you guide posing or let clients figure it out?"
- "Do you include hair and makeup? Outfit styling? Product design consultation?"

### Layer 2: The Emotional Transformation (How They Feel)
- "What do clients say AFTER the session that surprises you?"
- "What's the most emotional reaction you've ever gotten at a reveal?"
- "If I asked your best client why she chose you over the three other studios she was considering, what would she say?"
- "What change do clients describe about themselves after working with you?"

### Layer 3: The Proof (Why They Should Believe It)
- "How many clients have you photographed?"
- "What's your biggest single sale?"
- "What's the most impressive transformation you've seen?"
- "Any awards, features, certifications?"
- "What's the most specific compliment a client has given you in a review?"

## Step 2: The "So What?" Test

For every feature or differentiator the photographer mentions, run the "So What?" test:

**Feature:** "We have a wardrobe closet with 200 pieces."
**So what?** "So you don't have to stress about what to wear, don't have to buy anything, and walk in feeling prepared."
**USP version:** "Full luxury wardrobe included — 200+ hand-selected pieces so you walk in and feel incredible without buying a single thing."

**Feature:** "We include hair and makeup."
**So what?** "So you look and feel like the best version of yourself before a single photo is taken."
**USP version:** "Professional hair, makeup, and styling included — you show up, we handle the rest."

**Feature:** "I guide every pose."
**So what?** "So even if you've never been in front of a camera, every angle is directed and you look amazing in every frame."
**USP version:** "Zero modeling experience needed — I guide every pose so you feel confident and look stunning."

Run this for every differentiator. The feature is what you have. The "so what" is why they care. The USP version is what goes in ads, on the website, and in the quiz.

## Step 3: The Objection-to-USP Bridge

Load `references/genre-objections.md` for the full objection list by genre.

For each top objection, check: does the studio have a specific answer that's BETTER than the generic response?

**Formula: Major objection + studio's specific answer = a reason to book with them and nobody else.**

Example:
- **Objection:** "I don't know what to wear / I don't own lingerie" (boudoir)
- **Generic answer:** "Don't worry, we've got you covered!"
- **This studio's answer:** "We have a curated wardrobe closet with 200+ pieces in sizes XS to 4X — silk robes, bodysuits, luxury lingerie, even oversized sweaters for a more covered look. Your stylist helps you pick outfits that match your comfort level and your vision."

The specific answer is the USP. The generic answer is what every studio says.

Map the top 5 objections to studio-specific answers. These become: quiz statement slides, ad copy, landing page sections, and phone script talking points.

## Step 4: Craft the USP Statement

Generate 3 options using this formula:

"[Studio Name] is the only [genre] studio in [market] that [specific differentiator], so you [specific benefit] — even if [biggest objection]."

Examples:
- "Evoke Boudoir is the only boudoir studio in Austin that includes a full luxury wardrobe, professional styling, and guided posing in every session — so you walk in nervous and walk out feeling like the most beautiful version of yourself, even if you've never been in front of a camera."
- "Tiny Miracles Newborn is the only newborn studio in Orlando with a dedicated safety-trained photographer, a climate-controlled studio, and sessions scheduled within 14 days of birth — so your baby's first portraits are safe, comfortable, and captured at the perfect age, even if your due date changes."

Let the photographer pick or combine. This statement drives everything downstream.

## Step 5: Offer Architecture

### The Photography Pricing Model

Photography studios have a unique pricing model that differs from most service businesses:

**Session Fee (the door):** $0-$499 — this gets them in the door. Lower = more volume. Higher = more qualified. Most successful IPS studios run $49-$199 session fees.

**Average Sale (the revenue):** $1,500-$5,000+ — this is where the money is made. Wall art, albums, collections, digital packages. Sold at the IPS reveal session.

**The Formula: "Give Away the Shoot, Keep the Money."**
A low session fee creates volume. The IPS experience creates revenue. The combination is what produces $30K+ months.

### Build the Offer

**Step 5a: The Session Offer (what the ad promotes)**

Name it. "Spring Portrait Experience" is forgettable. "The [Name] Experience" or "The [Theme] Collection Session" or seasonal campaigns (40 Over 40, Mother's Day Glow, Holiday Heritage) make it feel special.

Components to include:
- Session time and what's included
- Hair/makeup/styling (if applicable)
- Wardrobe access (if applicable)
- Number of final images or "custom collection" framing
- Location (studio, outdoor, client's home)
- Any bonus (print credit, mini album, digital download)

**Step 5b: The Offer Incentive (what makes them act NOW)**

Types that work for photography:
- Session credit / voucher ("$100 credit toward your session and products")
- Complimentary add-on (extra outfit change, additional person, mini album)
- Limited availability ("We take 8 newborn sessions per month")
- Seasonal theme with deadline ("Valentine's Boudoir — sessions available Feb 1-14 only")

**Step 5c: The Investment Context (set expectations without pricing on page)**

Never put full pricing on the landing page or in the ad. Instead, frame the investment:
- "Collections start at $[X]" (on the thank-you page, not the ad)
- "Most clients invest between $[X] and $[Y] in their portraits"
- "Payment plans available through PayPal Credit / Affirm"

This gets communicated on the thank-you page, in follow-up emails, and on the phone — not in the initial ad or quiz.

## Step 6: Offer Math (Revenue Formula)

Ground everything in real numbers:

```
Session fee:                    $[X]
Average IPS/reveal sale:        $[Y]
Total revenue per client:       $[X + Y]
Current booking rate:           [Z]%
Monthly lead target:            [from Q14 goal]
Bookings needed per month:      [revenue target / revenue per client]
Leads needed per month:         [bookings needed / booking rate]
Target cost per lead:           [monthly budget / leads needed]
Target cost per booking:        [monthly budget / bookings needed]
Break-even:                     [budget / revenue per client] = clients needed to cover ad spend
```

Present: "Based on your numbers, you need [X] booked sessions per month. At a [Y%] booking rate, that means [Z] leads per month. At $[A] per lead, your ad budget produces [B] leads and [C] bookings — generating $[D] in revenue from $[E] in ad spend."

If the math doesn't work (CPL target is unrealistically low), flag it and suggest adjustments — increase budget, improve booking rate, increase average sale, or add organic prospecting.

## Stage 2 Output: USP & Offer Framework (Markdown)

- USP statement (final version)
- Top 5 differentiators with "So What?" transformations
- Objection-to-USP bridge map (top 5 objections with studio-specific answers)
- Offer name and components
- Offer incentive
- Investment framing language
- Offer math breakdown
- Revenue Formula current vs. target
- Seasonal campaign suggestions (next 2-3 opportunities)

**Revenue Formula connection:** This stage improves Average Sale (offer structure, IPS framing) and sets up Leads (offer incentive drives ad response) and Booking Rate (investment context reduces sticker shock on the phone).

- [ ] Offer math calculated with real numbers
