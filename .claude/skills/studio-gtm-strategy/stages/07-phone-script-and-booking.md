# Stage 7: The 5-Stage Phone Script & Booking Flow

## Why the Script Matters

The phone call is where leads become revenue. A studio can have the best ads, the best quiz, the best follow-up — and lose everything on a bad call. One newborn studio was doing $500K/year, hit a slump of one booking per month, and blamed the leads. The leads were fine. The person answering phones had stopped following the script. We fixed the script adherence. Nine sessions booked in the first seven business days.

The phone script is the Booking Rate lever in the Revenue Formula. Improving it from 15% to 40% triples bookings without spending a single additional dollar on ads.

Load `references/phone-script.md` for the full script structure with genre-specific discovery questions.

## Pre-Call Ritual (Every Single Time)

Five minutes before every consultation call:

1. **Quiet room, no interruptions.** Not in the car. Not at the grocery store.
2. **Phone with headphones or speaker.** Hands free for notes.
3. **Record the call.** Ask permission: "I normally take a ton of notes, but I'd rather give you my full attention — do you mind if I record?" Almost nobody says no. Recording alone can double bookings because it forces you to confront what you actually sound like.
4. **Pen and notepad.** Physical. Not laptop.
5. **Close your laptop.** You're listening, not multitasking.
6. **Release attachment to the "yes."** The booking is a byproduct of understanding what they need. If you sound desperate, they'll feel it.
7. **Dial the moment the clock hits.** Not two minutes late.

**Energy matters more than words.** Sound sharp, enthusiastic, and genuinely excited to help. Flat energy = lost booking.

## The 5-Stage Script

### Stage 1: Build Rapport (30 Seconds)

Three questions. That's it.

"Hey [Name], how's your week going?"
"Where are you calling from today?"
"By the way — where did you find me?"

That last one is gold. It tells you which marketing channel is working. It also gets them talking about your work, which starts the emotional connection.

Don't skip this. Don't spend ten minutes on it either. Thirty seconds.

### Stage 2: State the Agenda and Take the Lead (30 Seconds)

This is where 90% of photographers lose the call. They let the client drive. The client asks "how much does it cost?" and the photographer answers. You just lost control.

Instead:

"Before we get started — I'd like to ask you a few questions about you and your vision for the session. If I think we're the right fit, I'll tell you exactly how to reserve your spot. Sound good?"

Read that again. "If I THINK we're the right fit." That's prestige. That's limited availability. That's leadership. You're the expert deciding if this is a match — not the other way around.

### Stage 3: Discovery (10-15 Minutes — THE MOST IMPORTANT STAGE)

The discovery IS the close. If you diagnose perfectly, the prescription is obvious and the prospect closes themselves.

**Genre-specific discovery questions:**

**Boudoir:**
- "What made you start thinking about a boudoir session?"
- "Have you ever done anything like this before?"
- "Is this for you, or is it a gift for someone?"
- "What would it mean to you to see yourself in those images?"
- "Is there anything about the experience that makes you nervous? I want to make sure I address it."
- "Have you thought about where you'd want to display the images — or is this more of a private keepsake?"

**Newborn/Maternity:**
- "When are you due? Congratulations!"
- "Is this your first? Tell me about your family."
- "Do you have any other little ones? What are their personalities like?"
- "Have you thought about what style you're drawn to — more lifestyle or more posed?"
- "Where in your home would you imagine hanging the portraits?"
- "Is there a family heirloom or meaningful item you'd want to include?"

**Family:**
- "Tell me about your family — how old are the kids?"
- "What are their personalities like? Who's the ham, who's the shy one?"
- "When was the last time you had professional photos done?"
- "What's the main reason you're thinking about it now?"
- "Where would these images live in your home?"
- "Is there anyone else who'd be in the session — grandparents, pets?"

**Wedding/Engagement:**
- "Congratulations! Tell me about your day — what's the vibe you're going for?"
- "What's most important to you about the photography?"
- "Have you been to any other weddings where you loved the photos? What stood out?"
- "What moments are non-negotiable for you to have captured?"
- "When's the date? And where's the venue?"

**Headshot/Branding:**
- "What do you need the images for — LinkedIn, website, marketing materials?"
- "What's the vibe of your brand? More corporate, more creative, somewhere in between?"
- "Do you need individual shots, team shots, or both?"
- "When do you need them by?"
- "Is there a specific look or feeling you're going for?"

**The key:** Ask, then LISTEN. Really listen. Write down their exact words. You'll mirror those words back in the prescription. When someone hears their own language reflected back, they feel understood — and people book with people who understand them.

**Future-pacing questions (use for every genre):**
- "Where in your home would you imagine these hanging?"
- "What would it mean to you to walk past that image every morning?"
- "If we could capture exactly what you're describing — what would that be worth to you?"

### Stage 4: Prescription (3 Minutes)

Keep this short. Discovery did the heavy lifting.

"Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd recommend..."

- Present the session experience using THEIR words, not your marketing language
- Reference the offer by name: "This is our [Offer Name / Studio Experience]"
- Walk through what's included (wardrobe, hair/makeup, posing guidance, reveal)
- State the session fee clearly
- Introduce products: "After the session, you'll come back for a private reveal where we go through your images together. Most clients invest between $[range] in their final collection — wall art, albums, prints. And we have payment plans available."

**Do NOT hide the IPS component.** Set expectations clearly. The people who hear "most clients invest $2,000-$4,000" and still want to book? Those are your clients.

### Stage 5: Close (2 Minutes)

**The Standard Close:**
"I'd love to get you on the calendar. I have availability on [date] and [date] — which works better for you?"

Offer two specific dates. Don't say "when are you free?" — that's too open-ended and delays the decision.

**The Prestige Close (for busy seasons or premium studios):**
"I only take [X] sessions per month because I want to give every client my full attention. I have [Y] spots left in [month]. Want to grab one?"

This isn't manufactured scarcity if it's true. If you actually have capacity limits, state them.

**If they say "I need to think about it":**
"Totally understand. What specifically are you weighing? I'd rather help you work through it now than have you leave with an unanswered question."

**If they say "it's too expensive":**
"I hear you. Let me ask — we do have payment plans. If we could break the session fee into [X] payments of $[Y], would that make it easier? And keep in mind, the session fee is just the starting point. Your collection at the reveal is completely up to you — there's no pressure to spend beyond what feels right."

**If they say "I need to ask my husband/partner":**
"Of course! What do you think they'll want to know? Let me give you everything for that conversation. Would it help if I sent a quick summary you can share? Or I'm happy to jump on a 5-minute call with both of you."

**If they say "I'm just not ready yet":**
"Totally fine. When do you think would be better? I'll set a reminder and reach out then. In the meantime, your $[X] credit doesn't expire. And I'll keep sending you behind-the-scenes looks at what we do — if the emails get annoying, just let me know!"

**If they still can't commit after all objections — Booking Event fallback:**
"No pressure at all. Actually, we're doing something a little different this month that might make this easier. I'm opening the studio for a private preview night on [date]. You'd get to watch a real session happen, see the images on the big screen, hold the finished products — all before you decide anything. Only [15] women, completely free to attend. Would that help you get a better feel for it?"

This is the last move before ending the call. It converts the undecided into the room rather than into the follow-up sequence. The event closes what the phone couldn't. If no event date is currently scheduled, say: "We do these periodically — want me to put you on the list for the next one?" That captures the lead without a false promise and creates a natural re-engagement trigger when the date is confirmed.

**If they say yes:**
"Amazing! Let's get you booked. I'm going to send you a booking confirmation with all the details — what to expect, how to prepare, and your session date. You'll also get a pre-session questionnaire so I can start planning your dream session. Sound good?"

Always end warm regardless of outcome. Non-bookers become referral sources and future clients.

## Booking Confirmation Page

- "You're booked! Here's what happens next."
- Session date and time
- What to bring (or "nothing — we provide everything")
- Pre-session questionnaire link (outfit preferences, inspiration, who's in the session)
- Add-to-calendar links
- Reschedule link
- Contact info for questions

## Reminder Sequence

**T-7 days (Email):** "Your session is next [day]! Here's everything you need to know..." Include prep tips, parking, what to expect.

**T-24h (Email):** "Tomorrow's the day! Here's your session details one more time." Restate time, location, what to bring.

**T-2h (SMS):** "Hey [Name]! Can't wait to see you in 2 hours. Studio is at [address]. See you soon!"

**T+5min no-show (SMS):** "Hey [Name], it looks like we missed each other! No worries — grab a new time: [RESCHEDULE LINK]"
