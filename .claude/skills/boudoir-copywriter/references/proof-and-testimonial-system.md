# Proof And Testimonial System

Use this file before and during boudoir website drafting.

## Rule 1: Proof before persuasion
For homepage, landing page, and service-page work, gather proof early.
Do not leave proof as an afterthought.

## What counts as proof
- reviews and testimonials
- review count or star rating
- verified client count
- privacy rules
- process clarity
- reveal / ordering model
- payment plans if true
- body diversity and age diversity evidence
- specific outcomes clients mention repeatedly

## Numeric-claim rule
Do not hedge unverified proof.
If “500+ women” is not confirmed, do not soften it to “hundreds.”
Either:
- verify it and use it clearly
- remove it
- replace it with another provable proof point
- mark it as TODO if the user wants a draft before confirmation

## Testimonial tagging system
Sort reviews by the emotional job they can do on the page.
Common boudoir tags:
- nervous / out of comfort zone
- wedding gift
- milestone birthday
- divorce / breakup / reinvention
- before surgery / after illness / body recovery
- body image healing
- guided posing
- hype woman / personal connection
- privacy / safety / professionalism
- diverse bodies / age inclusion
- “I wish I did this sooner”
- “I couldn’t believe that was me”

## Placement logic
Do not just drop reviews randomly.
Place them where they do a specific job:

- **Near the hero**: broad trust or “best decision” proof
- **After empathy**: nervous / awkward / out-of-comfort-zone proof
- **After process**: guided posing / no surprises / felt taken care of proof
- **After inclusions**: value, planning, or prep support proof
- **Before final CTA**: “do it” / “worth it” / “wish I had done it sooner” proof

## Pulling quotes from long reviews
Prefer short extracts that preserve the emotional arc:
- before: nervous, hesitant, unsure
- during: felt comfortable, guided, safe
- after: beautiful, empowered, worth it

Do not over-trim a quote until it loses meaning.

## When no testimonials are available
If proof is thin:
- say so briefly
- ask for reviews, screenshots, or pasted testimonials
- or use clearly labeled placeholders such as:
  - TODO: testimonial here about being nervous and then feeling comfortable
  - TODO: testimonial here about wedding gift turning into a gift to herself

The placeholder must specify the emotional job needed, not just “add testimonial here.”
