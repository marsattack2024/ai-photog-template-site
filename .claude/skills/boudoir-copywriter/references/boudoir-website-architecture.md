# Boudoir Website Architecture

Use this file for boudoir homepages, service pages, and landing pages.

## Core principle
Boudoir pages are trust pages first.
They do not convert by sounding luxurious alone.
They convert by making the buyer feel:
- understood
- safe
- guided
- not disqualified
- able to imagine herself following through

## Recommended homepage / service-page sequence
This is a strong default, not an absolute rule.

1. **Hero**
   - SEO-clear H1 with location + service keyword when relevant
   - emotional subhead
   - one-line reassurance
   - primary CTA
   - optional proof strip

2. **Empathy / tension section**
   - name the real hesitation
   - normalize nervousness
   - make the reader feel seen quickly

3. **Guide introduction**
   - introduce the photographer earlier than most generic sites do
   - in boudoir, trust in the person is part of the offer

4. **Why women book / emotional promise**
   - gift, milestone, healing, confidence reset, identity recovery
   - explain why this is bigger than “photos”

5. **How it works / what happens after inquiry**
   - remove fear of the unknown
   - show planning, session, reveal, and next step clearly

6. **What is included / value block**
   - feature -> benefit -> relief
   - explain what the buyer gets and why it matters

7. **Tailored-to-you section**
   - session customization, sets, styling, vibe options, flexibility

8. **Portfolio framing**
   - frame the gallery as proof for real women, not just aesthetics

9. **Social proof**
   - distribute proof rather than isolating it in one place only

10. **Identity / inclusion statement**
   - let the page say who this is for in an emotionally resonant way

11. **Crossroads section**
   - speak to postponement directly
   - “maybe later” is the real competitor

12. **Final CTA**
   - low-friction next step
   - explain what happens after the CTA

## Hero rules
- H1 should usually do the obvious job first: keyword + location + service clarity.
- Emotional tension can live in the subhead.
- Do not make the hero so safe that it sounds generic.
- Do not make the hero so objection-led that it feels like confirmation of inadequacy.
- Balance aspiration and recognition.

## Guide placement rule
If the photographer’s personality and trust are central to conversion, move the guide section up.
Do not bury the photographer near the bottom out of habit.

## Redundancy rule
On long-form pages, each section needs a distinct job.
If two sections both say “boudoir builds confidence,” merge or sharpen them.
Do not repeat the same benefit three different ways unless the repetition is doing different conversion work.

## Service page note
Boudoir service pages often behave like landing pages.
Do not write them like thin brochure pages.
They still need:
- empathy
- guide trust
- process
- proof
- reassurance
- CTA clarity
