# Angles Hooks And Ads

## What this file is for
Use this file when writing Meta ads, static ads, short-form hooks, campaign angles, carousel copy, retargeting concepts, or ad concepts tied to specific objections and desires.

## The one-angle rule
For short-form ads, choose one:
- one objection
- one desire
- one story
- one proof concept

Do not try to solve the entire boudoir decision in one ad.

## Core angle buckets

### 1. Weight-wait angle
Core thought:
- I’ll do it when I lose weight

What to emphasize:
- waiting does not create worthiness
- real women of every size do this
- posing, wardrobe, and lighting matter more than perfection

Sample hook patterns:
- Waiting to lose weight before booking boudoir?
- You do not need to lose 20 pounds to do a boudoir shoot.
- The women you think are “ready” usually felt exactly like you.

### 2. Everyday woman angle
Core thought:
- those women are models, not people like me

What to emphasize:
- ordinary clients
- body diversity
- age diversity
- client stories that begin with fear

Sample hook patterns:
- These are not models. They’re women who almost didn’t book.
- Real bodies. Real nerves. Real transformations.
- You do not need model confidence to leave feeling incredible.

### 3. Expectation-gap angle
Core thought:
- I thought this would be awkward / terrifying

What to emphasize:
- fun surprise
- comfort and laughter
- easy guidance

Sample hook patterns:
- Everyone thinks boudoir will feel awkward. Then this happens.
- The biggest surprise? How much fun it actually is.
- Nervous walking in. Glowing walking out.

### 4. Privacy angle
Core thought:
- what if someone sees the photos

What to emphasize:
- written consent
- complete control
- private experience

Sample hook patterns:
- Boudoir can be 100% private.
- Worried someone will see your photos? Here’s how that actually works.
- Nothing gets shared without your say-so.

### 5. Process-made-easy angle
Core thought:
- I do not know how this works and that scares me

What to emphasize:
- hair and makeup
- wardrobe help
- guided posing
- reveal process

Sample hook patterns:
- What a boudoir session actually looks like
- You literally just show up. Here’s what we handle.
- How to do a boudoir shoot in three easy steps

### 6. Permission / self-investment angle
Core thought:
- this feels selfish / I need a better reason

What to emphasize:
- no milestone required
- wanting to feel like yourself again is reason enough
- self-investment is not frivolous

Sample hook patterns:
- You do not need a birthday, breakup, or bridal season to do boudoir.
- Wanting to feel like yourself again is enough.
- No milestone? No problem. Existing is enough.

### 7. Milestone angle
Core thought:
- I’m entering a new chapter and want to mark it

What to emphasize:
- chapter change
- courage evidence
- celebration with meaning

Sample hook patterns:
- Big birthday. Big chapter. Big “why not now?” energy.
- Divorce, remission, promotion, reinvention — this is how you mark it.
- The milestone is real. Your photos should be too.

### 8. Gift angle
Core thought:
- I want to do this as a gift, but I need it to feel meaningful

What to emphasize:
- album / print products
- partner reaction if true
- confidence is still the deeper win

Sample hook patterns:
- He gets the album. You get the confidence.
- The gift may be for him. The transformation is for you.
- Best gift idea: unforgettable for him, life-giving for you.

### 9. Review-proof angle
Core thought:
- I need evidence from other women, not marketing copy

What to emphasize:
- review phrases
- testimonials
- repeated emotional themes

Sample hook patterns:
- “I was so nervous but it was the best experience of my life.”
- At some point, it stops being marketing and starts being proof.
- Same sentence in review after review: “I wish I’d done it sooner.”

## Hook library starter bank
Use these as openers, cover text, first lines, or scroll-stoppers.

- Before you talk yourself out of boudoir again, read this.
- I almost cancelled my boudoir session three times.
- If you think you need to lose weight first, this is for you.
- You are not too old. You are overdue.
- What a boudoir session actually looks like.
- The women on our page are regular women. Not models.
- You do not need confidence to book. Sometimes booking is how confidence starts.
- I did this as a gift for him. It ended up healing something in me.
- I thought it would be awkward. I ended up having the best time.
- The right time was never “after I fixed myself.”
- You can keep waiting for the “perfect” body, or you can finally do the thing.
- Every woman says some version of this before booking: “I’m interested, but…”
- The first thing almost every client says after her session? “Why did I wait so long?”
- Boudoir is not about being fearless. It is about doing something brave while scared.
- If you need a sign, this is the one.

## Static ad guidance
For static ads:
- make the visual and headline match exactly
- use body-diverse imagery when the message is body confidence
- use BTS or team imagery when the message is comfort / process
- use product images when the message is gift / tangible value
- use before-and-after or review screenshots when the message is proof

## Carousel guidance
Use carousels to distribute objections or process steps, not to dump paragraphs.

### Objection carousel sequence
1. cover: the big promise or big question
2. slide 2: one fear
3. slide 3: the reframe
4. slide 4: a second fear
5. slide 5: the reframe
6. slide 6: “what’s included” or “how it works”
7. slide 7: proof / testimonial
8. slide 8: privacy or control
9. slide 9: emotional payoff
10. slide 10: CTA

### Retargeting carousel themes
- “What if I don’t know how to pose?”
- “What do I wear?”
- “What if I’m too old?”
- “What if I hate the photos?”
- “Where do the images go?”
- “How private is this?”

## Retargeting copy guidance
Retargeting should do at least one of the following:
- answer a specific objection
- show a real woman’s story
- explain the process visually
- reduce financial or privacy anxiety
- make the next step feel simple

Retargeting subject / headline style examples:
- Still thinking about boudoir?
- Worried you won’t know what to do?
- The number one thing almost every client is scared of
- Let’s talk about privacy for a second
- What happens after you inquire

## Ad deliverable rules
When the user asks for multiple ads:
- do not produce eight near-duplicates
- label the angle for each ad
- make the hook, body logic, and CTA meaningfully different
- vary the emotional driver, not just synonyms
