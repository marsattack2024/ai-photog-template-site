# Brand Tension Dial

Use this file to choose how hard the copy should lean into fear, vulnerability, desire, and emotional risk.

## Why this matters
Boudoir copy gets flat when it is too safe.
It also gets clumsy when it pushes emotional tension too hard.
Choose the tension level intentionally.

## Modes

### 1. Softer
Use when the studio voice is elegant, gentle, premium, or especially conservative.

Characteristics:
- more reassurance than challenge
- more elegance than edge
- emotional, but not confrontational
- safer hero language

Example feel:
- for the woman who wants to feel beautiful again
- private, guided, luxury experience

### 2. Balanced
Default mode when no stronger preference is given.

Characteristics:
- names real hesitation
- still leads with aspiration or clarity
- enough emotional edge to feel alive
- objection language usually lives in the subhead or empathy section

Example feel:
- boudoir for the woman who doesn’t feel ready yet
- you do not need confidence before you book

### 3. Emotionally risky
Use when the brand is bold, intimate, raw, hypey, or intentionally provocative.

Characteristics:
- more direct naming of postponement, self-criticism, fear, or desire
- stronger “stop waiting” energy
- more midnight-thought language
- still grounded in reassurance and proof

Example feel:
- for the woman who keeps telling herself maybe after I lose the weight
- you are not booking this because you feel fearless. you are booking because you are tired of postponing yourself.

## Selection rule
For website copy, do not choose this silently when the answer is unclear.
If the current page or the user’s comments show a strong preference, follow it.
If not, ask a quick question such as:
- Do you want this page to feel softer, balanced, or keep more emotional edge?

## Guardrails
- Do not flatten edgy brands into generic luxury copy.
- Do not overcorrect into negativity at the top of the page.
- Keep SEO-clear headings when needed; let emotional tension live in subheads or body copy if necessary.
