# StoryBrand Framework

## What this file is for
Use this file for long-form copy, section structure, page rewrites, and any deliverable where the message needs a clear emotional arc.

## The StoryBrand adaptation for boudoir

### The client is the hero
She is not trying to “buy photography.”
She is trying to solve an emotional tension:
- I want to feel beautiful again.
- I want proof that I am still in there.
- I want to do something brave.
- I want to celebrate a milestone.
- I want to stop postponing this.

### The studio is the guide
The studio’s role is not to be the hero.
The studio should appear as:
- experienced
- calming
- professional
- privacy-conscious
- body-safe
- process-clear
- warm but authoritative

### The problem has layers
Use all three when helpful.

#### External problem
- I don’t know how to pose.
- I don’t know what to wear.
- I’m worried about privacy.
- I’m worried I’ll hate the photos.

#### Internal problem
- I don’t feel confident enough.
- I don’t feel like myself anymore.
- I’m scared this will confirm my insecurities.

#### Philosophical problem
- women should not have to earn permission to feel beautiful
- confidence should not be reserved for one body type, age, or life phase

## Default messaging spine

### 1. Headline
Address the central desire or tension.
Examples:
- Feel Beautiful And Confident With Private Boudoir Sessions
- You Don’t Need To Be “Ready” To Do Boudoir
- Finally See Yourself The Way Everyone Else Does

### 2. Subheadline
Clarify the mechanism or reassurance.
Examples:
- Guided, private boudoir sessions designed for real women who want to feel comfortable and stunning.
- Professional posing, hair and makeup, and a fully private experience that makes it easy to say yes.

### 3. Problem / empathy block
Show that the studio understands the hesitation in the client’s own language.

### 4. Guide / authority block
Show why this studio is safe to trust.
Use credentials, experience, proof, policies, team, reviews, inclusions, or process transparency.

### 5. Plan block
Break the experience into simple steps.
The unknown is the enemy.

### 6. Success block
Paint the after state.
Use identity outcomes, not only image outcomes.

### 7. Failure-to-act contrast
Show the cost of waiting or continuing to postpone.
Do this gently; do not shame.

### 8. CTA block
Give a clear and emotionally safe next step.

## Boudoir-specific StoryBrand rules
- empathy should sound like a woman talking to another woman, not a sales page robot
- direct objections are often more persuasive than abstract brand statements
- process clarity is part of the “plan” and must be tangible
- privacy and control are often as important as beauty and confidence
- “for someone like me” proof should appear before the user has to hunt for it

## Objection-led passage pattern
For genre- or objection-specific pages, this simple structure works well:

1. Name the concern.
2. Acknowledge the emotion behind it.
3. Explain exactly how the studio reduces or solves it.
4. Tie the solution to the transformation.
5. Invite the next step.

Example skeleton:
“Worried you won’t know how to pose? You are not expected to. We guide every angle, expression, and movement so you can relax and focus on feeling present. Most clients walk in nervous and leave saying it was far easier and more fun than they expected.”

## Common boudoir page promises that fit this framework
- private and discreet
- body-safe and judgment-free
- fully guided
- hair and makeup included if true
- wardrobe support included if true
- everyday women welcome
- meaningful products and reveal experience
