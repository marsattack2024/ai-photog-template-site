# Risk Reversal And Reassurance

Use this file for boudoir homepages, landing pages, service pages, FAQs, and objection handling.

## Principle
Reduce risk without planting new fears.
Boudoir is intimate and emotionally loaded. Some classic direct-response guarantees can backfire here.

## Preferred order of reassurance
1. **Process reassurance**
   - guided posing
   - prep support
   - clear booking flow
   - reveal explanation
2. **Privacy reassurance**
   - written consent before sharing
   - private studio
   - buyer control over images when true
3. **Proof-based reassurance**
   - ordinary women saying they were nervous and then felt amazing
4. **Soft risk-reversal**
   - you only purchase what you love
   - low-pressure consult
   - clear next steps after inquiry
5. **Hard guarantee language**
   - only use if the policy is real and user-confirmed

## Soft risk-reversal examples
Use when true:
- You only purchase what you love.
- Nothing is shared without your written consent.
- We guide every pose.
- You’ll know exactly what to expect before session day.
- Reaching out is low-pressure and simply the first step.

## Hard guarantee rule
Do not invent:
- reshoot promises
- satisfaction guarantees
- minimum-photo guarantees
- refund promises

Only use hard guarantee language if the user confirms the studio actually offers it.

## Risky copy rule
Emotionally risky copy is allowed when it matches the brand, but do not use risky phrasing that makes the buyer imagine failure.
Good risk names the tension she already feels.
Bad risk introduces a new worry.

## FAQ usage
FAQ answers should not sound robotic.
They should reassure in the same tone as the rest of the page.
Each FAQ should reduce hesitation, not merely provide information.
