# Personas And Journey

## What this file is for
Use this file when deciding who the copy is for, what trigger caused the lead to pay attention, and what message angle is most likely to convert.

## The five core boudoir personas

### 1. The New Mom
Typical age range:
- roughly 25 to 40

Core identity tension:
- she misses herself
- her body has changed
- she feels needed by everyone and seen by no one

Typical triggers:
- postpartum body changes
- birthday / anniversary
- seeing another mom’s transformation story

Big fears:
- my body will never look the same
- I won’t recognize myself in a good way
- this is selfish when I should spend on my family

Messages that land:
- you are allowed to celebrate the body that carried life
- you don’t need to “bounce back” to feel beautiful
- this can be the day you feel like yourself again

### 2. The Milestone Celebrator
Typical age range:
- wide range, often 30 to 60+

Core identity tension:
- she is stepping into a new chapter and wants to mark it

Typical triggers:
- divorce recovery
- a big birthday
- cancer remission or health recovery
- promotion or personal reinvention

Big fears:
- the photos won’t match how powerful I want to feel
- I’ll chicken out before I do something this bold

Messages that land:
- this is how you mark the chapter
- brave women deserve evidence of their courage
- the milestone isn’t only the event; it’s who you are becoming

### 3. The Gift-Giver
Typical age range:
- roughly 25 to 50

Core identity tension:
- she may enter through “this is for him” but often secretly needs it for herself

Typical triggers:
- anniversary
- Valentine’s Day
- wedding gift
- birthday / holiday gift

Big fears:
- my partner will think it’s weird or silly
- I’ll spend money on something I can’t use
- I’m only doing this because I need a gift idea

Messages that land:
- he gets the album, you get the confidence
- the gift may start for him, but the transformation is yours
- this is a deeply personal and memorable gift, not a gimmick

### 4. The Confidence Seeker
Typical age range:
- roughly 35 to 65

Core identity tension:
- she has been battling low self-esteem or body shame for a long time

Typical triggers:
- seeing someone like her in a testimonial or before/after
- following the studio for months or years
- a low point emotionally

Big fears:
- what if even professional photos can’t make me feel beautiful
- what if I leave feeling exactly the same

Messages that land:
- the women who say this most often are the same women who later say it changed how they see themselves
- you don’t need confidence to book; many women find confidence through the process

### 5. The Repeat Client
Typical age range:
- roughly 30 to 55

Core identity tension:
- very little resistance compared with first-timers; she wants novelty, expression, and the feeling again

Typical triggers:
- new sets
- new season
- another milestone
- desire to revisit the high

Big fears:
- less about fear, more about freshness and exclusivity

Messages that land:
- new concept, new set, new version of you
- if the first session unlocked something, this one lets you play with it

## The buyer decision journey

### Stage 1: The Scroll / Awareness moment
What she is doing:
- casually scrolling
- not “shopping” yet
- reacting emotionally to imagery, hooks, reviews, and “women like me” proof

What she needs:
- identification
- intrigue
- emotional interruption
- permission to imagine herself doing it

Best message types:
- 3 AM thought hooks
- body-confidence angles
- expectation-vs-reality ads
- testimonial clips
- review-based proof

### Stage 2: Consideration / Quiet research
What she is doing:
- reading reviews
- stalking the website
- wondering if this is safe, real, private, and worth it

What she needs:
- reassurance
- process clarity
- privacy clarity
- body diversity proof
- studio legitimacy

Best message types:
- FAQ ads
- “how it works” videos
- testimonials answering objections
- website pages that clearly explain the experience

### Stage 3: Inquiry / Consultation decision
What she is doing:
- deciding whether to fill the form, book the call, or hit send

What she needs:
- low-pressure CTA
- simple next step
- confidence that she won’t be pressured or embarrassed
- confidence that this is financially and emotionally safe

Best message types:
- consultation page copy
- reassurance emails
- direct response objection handling
- “here’s what happens next” messaging

### Stage 4: Booked but nervous
What she is doing:
- second-guessing
- worrying about wardrobe, body, time, and awkwardness

What she needs:
- calming process reminders
- practical guidance
- emotional reassurance
- reminder that nervous is normal

Best message types:
- pre-session email / SMS
- welcome packet language
- “what to expect” content
- testimonial snippets that mirror her current fear

### Stage 5: Post-session / Reveal / Advocacy
What she is doing:
- emotionally high
- seeing herself differently
- excited to talk if prompted well

What she needs:
- capture momentum
- testimonial prompts
- product framing
- referral / repeat session opportunities

Best message types:
- testimonial capture prompts
- reveal copy
- referral asks
- product reveal language

## Matching persona to message angle

- New Mom -> self-return, body grace, celebration without waiting
- Milestone Celebrator -> courage, chapter marker, new beginning
- Gift-Giver -> meaningful gift + personal transformation
- Confidence Seeker -> woman-like-me proof, nervous-to-confident story, safety and guidance
- Repeat Client -> novelty, exclusivity, expansion, creative play

## Matching stage to CTA

- awareness -> see how it works / watch / learn more / peek inside
- consideration -> read reviews / view what’s included / see if this is for you
- inquiry conversion -> book your consultation / apply / reserve your call
- booked -> check your prep guide / reply with questions / choose your favorites
- advocacy -> share your story / refer a friend / plan your next session
