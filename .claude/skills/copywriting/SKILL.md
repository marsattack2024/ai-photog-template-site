# Photography Website Copywriting

Use this for client-facing studio website copy. Write for the person booking the
session, not for photographers buying marketing, coaching, software, or agency
services.

## Source Hierarchy

1. User-provided client brief, real reviews, pricing, locations, and services
2. Existing page copy in this repo
3. Genre psychology and objection patterns
4. Operator-facing marketing docs only as background context about photographers
   as business owners. Do not leak agency, coaching, software, or lead-generation
   positioning into client-site copy.

## Default Audience

The buyer is a local client considering a photography session. Depending on the
site, this may be a boudoir client, bride, family, expecting parent, personal
brand client, senior, or creative-service buyer.

## Copy Principles

- Lead with transformation, identity, memory, confidence, or relief.
- Close with concrete logistics: what is included, where it happens, how to
  inquire, what happens after the form.
- Name the hesitation before the reader has to ask.
- Use local trust signals: city, neighborhoods, venue familiarity, review count,
  years in business, studio/private location, or service radius.
- Keep copy specific and plain. Avoid "luxury experience" unless the page shows
  what that means.
- Use CTA language tied to the action: `Check Availability`, `Book My Session`,
  `Start My Inquiry`, `See The Experience`.

## Genre Notes

- Boudoir: safety, privacy, posing guidance, body confidence, no-model-needed,
  image control, partner/gift optional but not the core promise.
- Wedding: trust, timeline confidence, editorial eye, full-day storytelling,
  calm presence, real gallery proof.
- Family: ease, warmth, kids being kids, generational value, low-stress session.
- Newborn/maternity: safety, softness, timing windows, guided preparation.
- Branding/headshots: confidence, authority, guided direction, usable image set.

## Page Structure

- Hero: one clear promise, specific audience/location, CTA, proof cue
- Empathy: why booking this can feel intimidating or easy to delay
- Experience: simple steps and what the client does next
- Proof: testimonials, publication badges, review count, galleries
- Offer: service/package framing without burying price-sensitive details
- FAQ: real objections, not filler
- Final CTA: restate outcome and reduce risk

## SEO Add-On

For full page copy, include:

- Meta title under 60 characters where possible
- Meta description under 155 characters where possible
- Suggested H1
- 3-6 FAQ questions suitable for FAQ schema
- Image alt-text guidance for hero/gallery assets

## Self-Review

Before finalizing copy, check:

- It sells the outcome before the mechanics
- It does not sound like a business selling marketing help to photographers
- CTAs are specific
- The first screen says what the studio does and where
- FAQs answer real booking hesitation
