# Website research guide

When a website is provided, extract the details most useful for quiz conversion.

## Look for
- genre and audience
- tone of voice
- promise or transformation language
- booking CTA wording
- offers or promotions
- FAQs and objection handling
- session process
- styling, wardrobe, prep, hair and makeup, or guidance support
- privacy or consent language
- products such as albums, wall art, digitals, collections, payment plans
- testimonials or proof points
- differentiators that make the studio feel distinct

## How to use findings

- Turn repeated reassurance themes into question topics.
- Turn differentiators into answer explanations.
- Reuse brand-safe wording patterns, but do not copy large phrases.
- Prioritize facts the user can actually support.
- If the site is sparse, infer cautiously and mark assumptions in the input summary.
- When the site includes a usable offer, carry it through the title slide and offer slide.
- When the site does not include a clear offer, do not silently invent one.

## Common angle examples by genre

These are examples only. Use them only if they fit the website and user input.

- Boudoir: confidence, privacy, posing guidance, styling, hair and makeup, retouching, age, products
- Wedding or elopement: planning ease, comfort on camera, timeline help, album value, location expertise, booking process
- Newborn: timing, safety, baby-led pacing, prep help, family inclusion, products, stress reduction
- Maternity: scheduling window, wardrobe, comfort, styling, partner or children inclusion, finished artwork
- Family: outfit stress, uncooperative kids, relaxed experience, wall art, easy planning
- Branding: strategy, direction, image usage, confidence on camera, wardrobe guidance, professionalism
- Headshots: confidence, direction, expression coaching, brand fit, wardrobe, usage versatility
