# Build spec

## Hard rules

- Use original copy only.
- Build for Typeform.
- Include a title slide every time.
- Include a claim slide every time.
- Include an offer slide every time.
- Include a fair enough slide every time.
- Default to 4 main questions.
- Add 4 extra optional example questions.
- Keep statement slides between about 70 and 100 words when possible.
- Do not exceed 100 words per statement slide unless the user explicitly changes the rule.
- Mix answer formats when helpful. Standard multiple choice is the default, but select-all-that-apply, true-or-false, and occasional `all of the above` answers are allowed.
- For statement slides, do not use formulaic openers such as `The best answer is`. Start with a bolded headline-style transition and flow directly into the explanation.
- Keep the thank-you page short.
- Keep the follow-up email short.
- Keep the copy genre agnostic until the user's genre and website indicate otherwise.
- Annotations in reference files are for internal learning only and must never appear in client-facing output.
- Example files are inspiration only. Do not reuse wording closely.

## Strategy standards

- The main quiz should usually feel like a challenge the prospect wants to get right.
- At least 3 of the 4 main questions should usually have a clear best answer, even when the quiz includes select-all-that-apply, true-or-false, or `all of the above` structures.
- Prefer misconception, myth, objection, trust, process, timing, or value questions over preference questions.
- Do not default to survey-style prompts such as `what matters most to you` or `how do you feel about` for the main 4 questions unless the user explicitly requests that style.
- Focus each question on one strong objection, benefit, feature, myth, or desired outcome.
- Use questions to teach, reassure, and move the lead toward the offer.
- Prefer emotional reassurance plus practical clarity.
- Use the website for specificity.
- Do not invent offers, guarantees, turnaround times, deliverables, or policies as hard facts.
- If a claim is inferred, soften it with language such as `typically`, `often`, or `based on your site`.
- Keep wrong answers playful, not cringe or distracting.
- Use answer-type variety to improve rhythm, not to show off format tricks.
- When `all of the above` is genuinely the strongest answer, make the supporting options specific, meaningful, and tied to studio features or benefits.
- Write for conversion, not for cleverness.

## Statement slide pattern

Each statement slide should usually do most of the following in a tight paragraph:

1. Open with a short bolded headline-style transition, not `The best answer is`.
2. Name the fear, myth, or assumption.
3. Reveal the right or best answer naturally inside the paragraph.
4. Explain why that answer matters.
5. Tie the answer back to the studio's process, proof, or differentiators.
6. End with a subtle confidence-building push toward booking.

## Cross-genre rule

Keep the pattern consistent across genres.

- Boudoir may focus on privacy, posing, confidence, retouching, age, and products.
- Newborn may focus on safety, timing, preparation, family inclusion, stress reduction, and heirloom products.
- Maternity may focus on wardrobe, timing, comfort, family inclusion, and finished artwork.
- Branding or headshots may focus on strategy, guidance, confidence on camera, wardrobe direction, and image usage.
- Wedding may focus on planning, timeline help, candid direction, experience, and albums.

Change the substance by genre, not the underlying challenge-style format.

## Final quality check

Before delivering, verify that the quiz:
- sounds like the studio rather than a generic template
- reflects the genre and offer accurately
- uses 4 main questions and 4 extra example questions
- includes all required sections in the correct order
- keeps statement slides concise
- includes at least one privacy, preparation, value, or experience reassurance when relevant
- feels like a `can you get this right` educational challenge rather than a survey
- uses answer-format variety naturally when it improves the experience
- avoids formulaic statement-slide openings
- does not quietly invent or downgrade the offer
