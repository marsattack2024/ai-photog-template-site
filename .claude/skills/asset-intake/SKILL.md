# Asset Intake

Use this before heavy page building when the quality and placement of images
will determine the site quality. Output drives `siteConfig.images` and the
files that go into `/public/`.

## Workflow

1. **Inventory** provided assets by folder, filename, dimensions, format,
   and file size.
2. **Group by use** — match each candidate to the slots the template
   actually has:

   | Slot | Path in template | Where referenced |
   |---|---|---|
   | Hero | `/public/images/hero.{webp,jpg}` | `siteConfig.hero.imageSrc` |
   | Photographer portrait | `/public/images/portrait.{webp,jpg}` | `siteConfig.images.portrait` |
   | Image-quote breakers (3×) | `/public/images/quotes/{name}.webp` | `siteConfig.images.imageQuotes[].src` |
   | Gallery items | `/public/images/gallery/*.webp` | `GalleryGrid` (currently hand-listed in component; future: prop-driven) |
   | Session-type scene cards | `/public/images/scenes/*.webp` | `SessionScenes` props (if used) |
   | Testimonial headshots (optional) | `/public/images/headshots/*.webp` | `content.config.ts` → `featuredTestimonials[].photoSrc` |
   | OG card | n/a — auto-generated by `app/opengraph-image.tsx` | (no asset needed) |
   | Favicon | n/a — auto-generated by `app/icon.tsx` from brand initial | (no asset needed) |

3. **Flag image risks** before they get placed:
   - **Too small** — hero needs ≥ 2000px wide; image-quote breakers ≥ 1600px;
     portrait ≥ 1200px short edge; gallery ≥ 1600px on long edge
   - **Heavy file** — single image > 500KB unoptimized; > 200KB after WebP
     conversion is concerning
   - **Awkward crop** — subject too close to text-overlay zones (see hero
     composition note below)
   - **Wrong orientation** — gallery item that doesn't fit its masonry slot
   - **Duplicated pose / outfit** — kills variety
   - **Inconsistent editing style** — different photographer's mood mixed in
   - **Privacy concern** — model release status unclear; identifiable minors;
     boudoir without explicit consent
   - **Missing alt context** — can't describe what's in the frame
4. **Recommend exact placements** in `siteConfig.images` and `/public/` paths.
5. **Produce a missing-asset list** — only what blocks launch or weakens the
   first impression.

## Standards

### Format
- **Prefer WebP** for new images. Smaller payload than JPG at equivalent
  quality, supported by every modern browser.
- **JPG acceptable** for delivered client images that aren't easily
  re-exported. `next/image` will convert to WebP/AVIF at request time, but
  source size still affects build output and edge cache.
- **PNG only** for logos/SVG-impossible graphics with transparency. Photos
  should never be PNG.
- **SVG** for logos and icon-style art; never for photos.
- **No HEIC.** Convert before drop-in.

### Dimensions + aspect

| Slot | Recommended dimensions | Aspect | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | 2400 × 1500 (or larger) | ~16:10 to 3:2 | Covers ultra-wide screens; subject needs to crop well at 16:9 desktop AND 9:16 mobile |
| Portrait | 1200 × 1500 | 4:5 | MeetPhotographer renders aspect-[4/5] |
| ImageQuote breaker | 1800 × 1000 | 16:9-ish | Renders at 380px–620px tall; needs detail at 2× display |
| Gallery item | varies | mix of portrait/landscape | GalleryGrid uses intentional per-image heights (masonry) |
| SessionScenes card | 1200 × 1200 | 1:1 | Renders in fixed 80-tall cards, object-cover |
| Testimonial headshot | 400 × 400 | 1:1 | Renders 96px circular; needs face centered |

### Hero composition (image-first principle)
- **Subject must NOT sit where the headline lands.** On the template, hero
  text is centered on mobile (bottom 1/3) and left-aligned on desktop. If
  your hero image has the subject filling the bottom-left, text will overlap.
- **Pick images with editorial negative space** on one side OR top — let
  the photographer's frame do the work.
- **Mobile is the hard case.** A landscape hero with subject on the right
  works on desktop but the subject lands mid-frame on mobile. When text +
  image conflict on mobile, REDUCE TEXT (hide eyebrow, drop subline, hide
  secondary CTA) before re-cropping the image. The image is the asset.

### Alt text (this is SEO, not a formality)
- **Describe what's in the frame**, not "image of" or "photo of."
- **Include the subject's identity if relevant** — "Bride in cream slip
  dress laughing in morning light" beats "wedding photo."
- **Mention the studio / city if it serves discovery** — only on the hero
  image and gallery cover, not every gallery thumb (becomes spam).
- **40–120 chars** is the sweet spot. Don't try to game keywords.
- Examples of good alt text:
  - ✅ "Portrait session — woman in red lingerie reclining against a brick wall in soft afternoon light"
  - ✅ "Three generations of women laughing together during a family lifestyle session"
  - ❌ "Image of woman" (uninformative)
  - ❌ "Best boudoir photographer Seattle Washington luxury sessions book today" (spam)

### Variety in galleries
- Mix close, wide, detail, vertical, horizontal, emotional, polished final.
- 9–12 hand-picked > 30 medium ones.
- No duplicated pose or outfit unless it's a deliberate transformation arc.

### Privacy / consent
- Boudoir, milestone, family-with-kids: verify model release status before
  publishing.
- Avoid unintentional crops that compromise dignity.
- Children's faces require explicit parental consent on the release.

## Naming convention

```
/public/images/
  hero.webp                       # singular; the homepage hero
  portrait.webp                   # singular; photographer headshot
  quotes/                         # 3 image-quote breakers
    {keyword}.webp                # e.g. quotes/intimate.webp
  gallery/                        # gallery grid items
    {sequence-or-keyword}.webp    # e.g. gallery/01-bride.webp
  scenes/                         # SessionScenes cards (if used)
    {session-type}.webp           # e.g. scenes/bridal.webp
  headshots/                      # testimonial portraits (optional)
    {client-first-name}.webp      # e.g. headshots/sarah.webp
```

Keep filenames lowercase + hyphenated. Never use spaces or special chars.

## Output

When done, report:

- **Asset map by section** — which file goes where in siteConfig + on disk
- **Best hero candidates** — top 1-2 with reasoning (subject placement, mood)
- **Images to avoid** — and why
- **Missing assets** — what's still needed to launch (portrait? specific
  session type for the breaker? gallery variety?)
- **Alt text direction** — drafted alt for the heroes/breakers (gallery alts
  can be templated)
- **Compression / conversion recommendations** — what to re-export as WebP,
  what to resize
- **siteConfig.images patch** — the exact JS object to drop into
  `lib/site.config.tsx` once the photographer's images are in place
