# Bucketing Guide — Studio Reactivation

## The Four Buckets

Every response to the reactivation blast falls into one of four buckets. Sort immediately when responses come in. The speed of your response determines whether a cheetah books or goes cold again.

---

## Bucket 1 — Direct Yes

**Signals:** "Yes, I'm ready." / "I've been meaning to call." / "Can we book?" / "What dates do you have?"

**What to do:**
Move to consultation immediately. Call or text back within 2 hours of response — ideally within 15 minutes. These are cheetahs. They had intent when they first inquired and that intent just resurfaced. The window closes fast.

**CRM stage:** Move to "Consultation Scheduled" or "Follow-Up 1" depending on whether you've spoken yet.

**Do not:** Send them to an event. Do not run a nurture sequence. Close them now.

---

## Bucket 2 — Maybe / Soft Yes

**Signals:** "I'm still thinking about it." / "The timing is almost right." / "Tell me more." / "I've been meaning to reach out." / "Maybe." / "I need to check with my husband." / "I've been saving up." / Any response that isn't a yes or no.

**What to do:**
This is the largest bucket and the highest-leverage group. They showed interest — they replied. They just need a lower-friction next step than a phone consultation.

Decision fork:
- If the studio is running a Group Bonanza preview event → send the event invitation sequence (copy-templates.md, Event Invitation section)
- If no event → run Stage 4B standard maybe sequence (copy-templates.md, Standard Maybe Follow-Up)

**CRM stage:** Tag as "Maybe — Event Invited" or "Maybe — Nurture Sequence." Keep in active pipeline.

**Key rule:** Do not drop them from active follow-up just because they didn't say yes immediately. These are hares. They need more touches and a lower-barrier first step.

---

## Bucket 3 — Not Right Now

**Signals:** "I just had a baby." / "I'm in the middle of a move." / "Things are really crazy right now." / "Ask me in a few months." / Any clear timing objection without a no attached.

**What to do:**
Acknowledge, affirm, and release pressure completely. One brief warm response. Then move them to long-term nurture. Do not run an active sequence. They are tortoises — they will come back when the timing is right, but only if you haven't annoyed them into opting out.

**Text response:**
"Totally get it — life has its own schedule. I'll check back in a few months and I'll be here whenever you're ready."

**CRM stage:** Tag as "Long-Term Nurture." Set a 90-day CRM reminder to check in personally.

**Email list:** Keep them on weekly nurture emails. Value deposits only — no asks for 60 days.

---

## Bucket 4 — Hard No or No Response

**Hard no signals:** "I'm not interested." / "Please remove me." / "I already booked with someone else." / "This isn't for me."

**No response:** Did not reply after the initial text + a 3-day follow-up text + the email. Three attempts over 5 days with no response.

**What to do for hard no:**
Respond with grace. "No problem at all — I'll take you off my list. If you ever change your mind, I'm here." Honor opt-out requests immediately. Remove from active pipeline. If they asked to be removed from all contact, honor that.

**What to do for no response:**
Do not assume no. No response means not-yet. Move to email-only list. Keep them on weekly value content. Tag for quarterly reactivation blast with a new message and new angle.

**CRM stage:** Tag as "No Response — Email Only" or "Opted Out — Do Not Contact."

**Key rule:** Studios that delete no-response leads are leaving money on the table. Some of the highest-value bookings come from people who didn't respond to the first two contacts. Keep the list, change the approach, reduce the contact frequency.

---

## Long-Term Nurture System

**For Buckets 2 and 3 that don't convert in the initial campaign:**

Minimum touch cadence:
- Weekly: One value email (story, tip, testimonial, behind-the-scenes — not an ask)
- Monthly: One personal check-in text ("Hey [Name], just thinking of you — any closer to ready?")
- Quarterly: One reactivation blast with a fresh angle and seasonal hook

**Deposits vs. withdrawals rule:**
Every value email is a deposit. Every booking ask is a withdrawal. Do not make a withdrawal until you've made at least 4 deposits. Studios that blast offers every week to a cold list burn the list and train people to ignore them.

**Seasonal withdrawal calendar (maximum 3 per year):**
- Valentine's Day / early February
- Mother's Day / late April
- Fall / September

These are the three moments where a direct booking ask lands well. Outside of these windows, stay in deposit mode.

## CRM Tagging Structure (Generic — Adapt to Any CRM)

| Tag | Meaning | Next Action |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| HOT — Active | Replied yes or maybe, in active sequence | Contact within 2 hours |
| MAYBE — Event | Invited to preview event | Run event invite sequence |
| MAYBE — Nurture | In Stage 4B maybe sequence | Run 14-day sequence |
| NOT NOW — Nurture | Timing objection, no is not final | Monthly text, weekly email |
| NO RESPONSE | Did not reply after 3 attempts | Email only, quarterly reactivation |
| OPT OUT | Requested removal | Remove from all contact immediately |
| BOOKED | Converted | Move to pre-session sequence |

## Blast Volume Rules

Never send more than 200 texts per day from the same number. High-volume sends in a single day trigger carrier spam filters and can get the studio's number flagged or blocked. Spread the blast across multiple days.

Recommended cadence for large lists:
- 200 texts per day
- Matching email batch same day
- Monitor reply rates before sending the next batch — if replies are coming in faster than you can respond, pause and work the active conversations first

**Typical response windows:**
- Texts: 70% of responses come within 2 hours of send
- Emails: 60% of opens happen within 24 hours
- Check both channels morning and evening during active blast days

## Revenue Formula Benchmarks

When presenting the reactivation case to a studio, use this math:

2% conversion of existing leads × average sale = reactivation revenue

| Lead Pool | 2% of List | At $1,500 avg | At $2,500 avg | At $3,500 avg |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| 200 leads | 4 bookings | $6,000 | $10,000 | $14,000 |
| 500 leads | 10 bookings | $15,000 | $25,000 | $35,000 |
| 1,000 leads | 20 bookings | $30,000 | $50,000 | $70,000 |

These are conservative. Well-run reactivation campaigns with a warm list regularly produce 5-8% conversion on the maybe bucket specifically.
