# Genre Psychology

Use this reference to adapt the emotional stakes and objections by portrait genre. Always translate features into the buyer's felt problem and desired transformation.

## Boudoir

Core belief shift: I do not need to wait until I lose weight, feel braver, or look younger. My body now is worthy of being photographed safely and beautifully.

Primary hesitations:
- I need to lose weight first.
- What if I look terrible?
- My body has changed.
- What will people think?
- Will my photos stay private?
- I do not know how to pose.
- I have nothing to wear.
- I am too old.
- What will I do with the photos?

Copy requirements:
- Address body image, privacy, posing guidance, wardrobe, age, and safety.
- Use comfort, discretion, confidence, and self-reclamation language.
- Avoid male-gaze copy and oversexualized phrasing.
- Emphasize transformation: from nervous and self-critical to comfortable, powerful, and seen.
- Proof from real women matters more than studio claims.

## Newborn

Core belief shift: This stage is brief, and I can preserve it without adding more stress to my life.

Primary hesitations:
- What if the baby does not cooperate?
- Is the studio safe?
- When should I book?
- I feel overwhelmed.
- What if the baby is fussy?
- What should I bring?

Copy requirements:
- Emphasize safety, patience, calm, flexibility, and preparation.
- Name the time window without fearmongering.
- Encourage inquiry during pregnancy when relevant.
- Position artwork as proof of a stage parents cannot get back.

## Maternity

Core belief shift: My changing body is worth celebrating, and I will be guided into flattering, comfortable portraits.

Primary hesitations:
- Will I look good?
- I do not know how to pose.
- What should I wear?
- I feel self-conscious.
- When should I schedule?

Copy requirements:
- Use language around strength, anticipation, motherhood, glow, and transition.
- Mention wardrobe and styling support when true.
- Emphasize guidance and comfort.
- Avoid making the body sound like a problem to fix.

## Family

Core belief shift: My family does not need to be perfectly behaved to have portraits we will love for decades.

Primary hesitations:
- What if my kids do not cooperate?
- We have not had a good family photo in years.
- What should we wear?
- My partner does not want to do this.
- I am too busy.

Copy requirements:
- Make chaos feel normal.
- Focus on the season of life changing quickly.
- Mention styling guidance and child-led posing when true.
- Position wall art and albums as daily proof of belonging, not just products.

## Branding and Headshots

Core belief shift: I can look credible, current, and like myself without looking stiff or fake.

Primary hesitations:
- I am awkward on camera.
- I do not want a corporate-looking photo.
- I need images that match my brand.
- I need this quickly.

Copy requirements:
- Focus on credibility, trust, visibility, and alignment.
- Mention use cases: website, LinkedIn, speaker bio, press, team page, launch assets.
- Emphasize guided posing and expression coaching.
- Avoid vanity-first language.

## Senior

Core belief shift: This is not just a school photo. This is proof of who they were before everything changed.

Primary hesitations:
- Will it feel like them?
- What should they wear?
- Will it be awkward?
- Is this worth doing beyond school portraits?

Copy requirements:
- Mark the threshold between childhood and adulthood.
- Highlight personality, style, confidence, and family legacy.
- Speak to both parent and senior depending on page audience.

## Pet

Core belief shift: This bond matters, even if other people do not fully understand it.

Primary hesitations:
- My pet will not sit still.
- My pet is anxious or energetic.
- I want to capture their personality.
- I do not know how much time we have.

Copy requirements:
- Normalize movement and unpredictability.
- Emphasize patience, personality, and the human-animal bond.
- Respect grief and aging-pet urgency without exploiting it.
