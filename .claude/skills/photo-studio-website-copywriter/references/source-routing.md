# Source Routing

Use this reference to decide what to consult before writing.

## Always Relevant
- StoryBrand Copywriting for Photography Studios: use for hero, problem, guide, plan, CTA, objection handling, testimonial integration.
- Website Setup for Photographers: use for homepage, navigation, genre pages, about page, contact page, client resources, blog, CTAs, and long-scrolling layout.

## Conditional Sources

### Boudoir
Consult THE BOUDOIR BUYER BIBLE before writing or critiquing boudoir website, landing page, quiz, ad, email, or FAQ copy.

### Quiz or Popup
Consult Typeform quiz instructions when the page includes a quiz, popup, quiz CTA, objection-based quiz funnel, or Typeform implementation.

### Meta Ads to Landing Page
Consult Facebook Ads Handbook and carousel ad docs when the page must match Meta ad traffic.

### Google Ads to Landing Page
Consult Google Ads Handbook when the page must match search intent, RSA copy, or search campaigns.

### 40 Over 40 or 30 Over 30
Consult 40 Over 40 Campaign Handbook and FAQ for campaign flow, landing page sections, thank-you page expectations, deposit language, and follow-up logic.

### Email Follow-Up
Consult the Best Email Subject Lines Handbook when writing post-inquiry, campaign, or nurture email subject lines connected to the website/funnel.

## Source Hierarchy

When sources conflict, use this hierarchy:
1. Explicit user instruction.
2. Client-specific proof and offer details.
3. P2P operating rules.
4. Genre-specific P2P docs.
5. General StoryBrand and website docs.
6. General platform best practices.

Important P2P override: standard studio websites should not publish pricing unless the user explicitly instructs otherwise. Campaign landing pages may include deposits, offer terms, or pricing anchors when required for transparency.
