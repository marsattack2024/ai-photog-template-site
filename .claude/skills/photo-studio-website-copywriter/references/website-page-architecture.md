# Website Page Architecture

Use these structures as defaults. Adapt to the studio, genre, and offer.

## Homepage

Goal: clarity, trust, and routing.

Sections:
1. Hero: what, who, where, why it matters, CTA.
2. Emotional problem: name the reason the visitor has delayed.
3. Genre routing: separate cards for each service category.
4. Guide section: empathy first, authority second.
5. Experience plan: three steps maximum.
6. Portfolio preview: selected images with strategic captions.
7. Product/artwork positioning: albums, wall art, folios, printed legacy.
8. Proof: testimonials, reviews, press, screenshots, before/afters where appropriate.
9. FAQ preview: answer the biggest objections.
10. Final CTA: same conversion path as the hero.

Hero formula:
- Headline: desired transformation.
- Subheadline: who + problem solved + location.
- CTA: one action.
- Microcopy: reassurance or risk reducer.

## Genre Service Page

Goal: booking rate and qualified inquiries.

Sections:
1. Genre-specific hero.
2. Objection-aware opener: "You might be wondering..."
3. Why this matters now.
4. What makes this studio different.
5. The experience.
6. What is included, without dumping pricing unless campaign context requires it.
7. Three-step plan.
8. Relevant testimonials.
9. FAQ.
10. CTA.
11. Emotional close.

## Campaign Landing Page

Goal: convert traffic from ads, email, or quiz into leads/applications.

Rules:
- One campaign, one audience, one CTA.
- Match the ad language closely.
- Clarify the offer, eligibility, deposit, terms, and next step.
- Include proof and visuals early.
- Keep forms short and mobile-friendly.
- Use a unique thank-you page for tracking.

Default sections:
1. Campaign hero and offer.
2. Who it is for.
3. Why this campaign exists.
4. What is included.
5. How it works.
6. Proof/testimonials.
7. Application form.
8. Terms and expectations.
9. Final CTA.

## About Page

Goal: trust and emotional connection.

Sections:
1. Client-centered opener.
2. Photographer story only as it relates to the client.
3. Empathy.
4. Authority.
5. Philosophy.
6. Studio experience.
7. Invitation.

Never make the photographer the hero. The story should answer: "Why should I trust you with this moment?"

## Inquiry Page

Goal: reduce friction and increase form completion.

Sections:
1. Reassurance headline.
2. What happens after inquiry.
3. Short form.
4. Consent checkbox or permission-based language.
5. Expectation-setting.
6. Soft proof.
7. CTA.

Avoid budget questions, intimidating intake forms, and pricing interrogation.

## Thank-You Page

Goal: increase speed-to-lead and call show-up rate.

Sections:
1. Confirm submission.
2. Set expectations.
3. Invite next action such as scheduling a consult.
4. Add personal video or note when available.
5. Encourage portfolio/testimonial review.
6. Reinforce excitement.
7. Maintain a unique URL for conversion tracking.

## FAQ Section

Every FAQ should answer a real hesitation. Examples:
- What if I do not know how to pose?
- What if my kids do not cooperate?
- Will my photos stay private?
- What should I wear?
- When should I book?
- What happens after I inquire?
- Do I have to buy anything before seeing my images?
