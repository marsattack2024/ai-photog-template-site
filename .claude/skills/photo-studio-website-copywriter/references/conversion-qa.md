# Conversion QA Gate

Run this before delivering copy or audit recommendations.

## Hero Test

The hero must answer in three seconds:
- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- Where is it?
- Why should the visitor care?
- What should they do next?

## StoryBrand Test

- The client is the hero.
- The studio is the guide.
- Empathy comes before authority.
- The plan has no more than three steps.
- The CTA is direct and repeated.
- The stakes are specific and not manipulative.
- The success state is visual and emotional.

## Revenue Formula Test

Name which lever the page moves:
- Leads.
- Booking Rate.
- Average Sale.

If the copy moves none of these, rewrite it.

## Objection Test

The page must answer the dominant genre objection:
- Boudoir: body image, privacy, posing, wardrobe, age, cost/value.
- Newborn: safety, timing, fussiness, overwhelm.
- Maternity: posing, body confidence, wardrobe, timing.
- Family: kids, partner resistance, wardrobe, busy schedules.
- Branding: awkwardness, credibility, brand fit, speed.
- Senior: personality, wardrobe, awkwardness, value.
- Pet: cooperation, energy, personality, timing.

## Proof Test

- Claims are supported by proof or marked TODO.
- Testimonials appear near the objections they answer.
- Specific proof beats generic praise.
- Screenshots/raw reviews beat polished graphics when available.

## Mobile Test

- Short paragraphs.
- Clear section headers.
- CTA visible early.
- No giant form before emotional buy-in.
- FAQ answers are direct.
- Button copy is action-based.

## Studio Rules

- One genre per page.
- One primary CTA per page.
- No pricing on standard studio website pages unless explicitly overridden.
- Campaign pages may show deposit/terms when transparency is required.
- Do not send paid ad traffic to a homepage when a landing page is needed.
- Do not write generic copy that fits any industry.
- Do not invent proof.
